## Basic Structure Check

- Title direction: accurate, concise, clear, and not clickbait:
- Opening: starts close to the topic and states the purpose, question, scene, or core judgment early:
- Body layers: sections are arranged around the theme, with each section serving a distinct function:
- Focus and proportion: comprehensive context is covered briefly, while the main point gets the real space:
- Repetition control: repeated material is explained fully once and only referenced lightly elsewhere:
- Concept consistency: key terms keep the same meaning, and broad points are placed at the right level:
- Internal logic: problems raised earlier are answered later; solutions later have earlier setup:
- Ending direction: closing is concise and has conclusion, direction, recommendation, warning, encouragement, or open question:
