# course-study

> Turn a course into complete-coverage, Feynman-explained, exam-ready revision notes — nothing dropped.

**English** · [简体中文](README.md)

**What it does** — Turns a course's materials (PDF slides, a pasted topic list, or just a course name) into complete-coverage, Feynman-explained, exam-ready revision notes.

**Why it's good** —
- **Completeness is enforced**: a coverage checklist is emitted first, then reconciled line by line — nothing extracted is ever silently dropped.
- **Every concept gets a Feynman block**: capsule → intuition → formal → worked example → misconception; never lead with the formal definition.
- **Deliberately lean**: dropped quizzing, spaced-repetition, and Anki export to stay a clean revision tool.

**When to use** — "study/revise this course" · "revise these lecture slides" · "make a study guide / cheat sheet for my midterm/final/quiz" · "explain <course topic> simply"; or call `/course-study`.
**Not for** — album/music review (→ album-review); open-ended tutoring conversation; solving or doing your graded homework / exam questions to submit; a course with no materials AND no identifiable standard syllabus.

**Install** — `npx skills add VincentJiang06/skills` (or `cp -R skills/course-study ~/.claude/skills/`).
