# Chengdu

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 3-4 (3 for city highlights + Leshan day trip, 4 to add Emei Mountain)
- Best season: March–May (spring, mild and clear) and September–November (autumn, clearer skies; Chengdu is famously overcast year-round — truly blue skies are rare)
- Must-see: Giant Panda Base (arrive at 08:00), Kuanzhai Alleys, authentic Sichuan hotpot or 麻婆豆腐
- Best area to stay: Jinjiang District (锦江区) / Tianfu Square area — central, metro-accessible, 20min from Panda Base
- Key constraint: Giant Panda Base tickets sell out in peak season — book online at least 3 days ahead. Arrive by 08:30 when pandas are most active; they retreat indoors after 10:00.

## City Orientation

Chengdu is the capital of Sichuan Province and one of China's most livable cities — unhurried pace, exceptional food, strong tea-house culture, and an improbable concentration of giant pandas. It is also the gateway to Sichuan's dramatic landscapes (Leshan, Emei, Jiuzhaigou). The city itself is relatively flat and well-connected by metro.

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 13 lines. Alipay QR code at all gates. ¥2-7 per ride. Well-organized and covers most tourist sites.
- **To Panda Base**: Metro Line 3 to 熊猫大道站, then free shuttle bus or 15-min taxi ride. Total ~35min from city center.
- **Didi**: Works seamlessly throughout the city.
- **To Leshan**: 高铁 from 成都东站 to 乐山站 (~1h), then tourist bus to the Giant Buddha. Use `flyai search-train` for current schedules and prices.
- **To Emei**: 高铁 from 成都东站 to 峨眉山站 (~1-1.5h), then bus to the mountain base. Use `flyai search-train` for current schedules and prices.
- **To Qingcheng Mountain**: Metro Line 17 to 青城山站 (~1h) — the most convenient mountain day trip. Use `flyai search-train` for current schedules and prices.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Giant Panda Breeding Research Base (大熊猫繁育研究基地)
- **Time needed**: 2.5–3.5 hours
- **Reservation**: **MANDATORY during peak season** — book online at least 3–5 days ahead. Daily visitor quotas are enforced. Off-peak season: book 1–2 days ahead.
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 07:30–18:00 (last entry 17:00)
- **Tips**: Arrive at 08:00–09:00 sharp — giant pandas are fed in the morning and are most active for 1–2 hours after feeding. By 10:30, most have retreated indoors to sleep. Red pandas (小熊猫) are often more active in the afternoon and are located in a separate area. The base has 12+ giant pandas; Panda Cub Season (Aug–Nov) is when newborns are visible. No holding pandas (this practice has been banned).
- **Ticket**: ¥55

#### Kuanzhai Alleys (宽窄巷子)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; alleys are outdoor and always accessible
- **Hours**: Shops and restaurants 09:00–22:00; best before 10:00 or after 17:00
- **Tips**: Three parallel historic alleys (Wide Alley 宽巷子, Narrow Alley 窄巷子, Well Alley 井巷子) lined with Qing-dynasty courtyard houses now converted to teahouses, restaurants, and boutiques. Narrow Alley is the most atmospheric — traditional teahouses where locals play mahjong and have ear-cleaning done. Resting in a courtyard teahouse (¥30–50 for a pot of tea with bottomless refills) is the right pace for this area, not a brisk walk-through.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Wuhou Shrine + Jinli (武侯祠 / 锦里)
- **Time needed**: 2–3 hours combined
- **Reservation**: Not required for Jinli; Wuhou Shrine buy in advance or at gate
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Wuhou Shrine 08:00–18:00; Jinli Old Street 09:00–22:00
- **Tips**: Wuhou Shrine is a memorial to Zhuge Liang (诸葛亮) and Liu Bei — figures from the Three Kingdoms period (3rd century CE). The adjacent Jinli Old Street is an atmospheric reconstructed Qing-dynasty commercial street, less sanitized than Kuanzhai. Good for Sichuan street snacks. Visit Wuhou first, then walk directly into Jinli — they share an entrance area.
- **Ticket**: Wuhou Shrine ¥60; Jinli free

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Leshan Giant Buddha (乐山大佛) — Day Trip, UNESCO
- **Time needed**: Full day including transit (6–7 hours total)
- **Reservation**: Buy online in advance — peak season (May, Oct) quotas fill fast
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 07:30–19:30 (peak season) / 08:00–17:30 (off-peak)
- **Tips**: The world's largest stone Buddha (71m tall, carved 713–803 CE) carved into a cliff at the Minjiang River confluence. Two viewing options: the boat view from the river (best for scale — the full body is visible) or the descent along the cliffside path (close-up details, long queue). Do both if possible. The cliffside descent queue can be 1–2 hours. Arrive at opening. Combine with the old town of Leshan for lunch.
- **Ticket**: ¥80 (scenic area) + boat cruise additional

#### Qingcheng Mountain (青城山)
- **Time needed**: 4–6 hours including transit
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–18:00
- **Tips**: A Taoist mountain 65km from Chengdu with dense bamboo forests, ancient temples, and cable car access to the upper peaks. Two sections: Front Mountain (前山, more temples and scenery) and Back Mountain (后山, wilder and quieter). Front Mountain is recommended for a day trip. Metro Line 17 makes this the most accessible mountain from Chengdu — no car needed.
- **Ticket**: ¥90 (front mountain) + cable car ¥70 round trip

## Food & Dining

Chengdu is a UNESCO Creative City of Gastronomy and the origin of Sichuan cuisine. The flavor profile is 麻辣 (numbing-spicy from Sichuan peppercorns) but the cuisine is far more varied than hotpot alone.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Sichuan hotpot (四川火锅) | Spicy broth with Sichuan peppercorn; more numbing than Chongqing hotpot | 大龙燚 (major chain, reliable, English menu), local shops near Kuanzhai |
| Mapo tofu (麻婆豆腐) | Silken tofu in spiced ground pork and fermented black bean sauce; intensely numbing-spicy | 陈麻婆豆腐 (the original, near Qingyang) |
| Husband and wife slices (夫妻肺片) | Cold sliced beef and offal in chili oil and Sichuan peppercorn; the name refers to the dish's creators, not the ingredients | Any Sichuan restaurant |
| Dan dan noodles (担担面) | Thin noodles with sesame paste, chili oil, minced pork, and preserved vegetables; one of China's most imitated dishes | Street stalls and noodle shops |
| Zhong dumplings (钟水饺) | Steamed pork dumplings in sweet soy and chili sauce | 钟水饺 restaurant (chain); also in Jinli |
| Lai glutinous rice balls (赖汤圆) | Soft glutinous rice balls in sweet broth | 赖汤圆 restaurant; Jinli |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Kuanzhai Alleys**: Teahouses and upscale Sichuan restaurants; higher prices, good quality
- **Jinli**: Sichuan snack food — best for grazing rather than sitting-down meals
- **Yusanjie (玉林街道)**: Local residential area with excellent value authentic Sichuan restaurants; 20-min Didi from center

## Practical Tips for Chengdu

- **Chengdu is chronically overcast**: The Sichuan Basin traps humidity — blue sky days are genuinely rare. Do not plan outdoor activities expecting clear skies; most days have low cloud. This does not affect the experience significantly
- **Sichuan peppercorn numbing**: The 麻 (numbing) sensation from Sichuan peppercorn is different from chili heat — it temporarily numbs the mouth and lips. This is intentional and normal; it typically peaks and fades within 20 minutes
- **Hotpot is a 2-hour meal**: Sichuan hotpot is a social dining ritual, not a quick meal. Budget 1.5–2 hours minimum. Order raw ingredients gradually rather than all at once
- **Panda Base midday**: After 10:30, most pandas are sleeping and largely invisible. There is no point arriving after 10:00 if seeing active pandas is the goal
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable

## Gotchas
- **Panda Base sells out**: During Golden Week and summer, tickets sell out days in advance online and the physical queue is hours long. Book before you arrive in China if visiting peak season
- **Leshan cliffside queue**: The descent path for a close-up view of the Giant Buddha can have 1.5–2 hour queues on weekends. Use timed entry or the boat cruise as the primary view
- **Hotpot spice levels**: Ordering 微辣 (mildly spicy) in Chengdu still means significantly spicy by international standards. 不辣 (no spice) is an option at most restaurants. Specify clearly
- **Emei Mountain summit weather**: Even in summer, the 3,099m summit can have temperatures below 10°C and sudden rain or cloud. Bring a layer and rain gear regardless of the city weather
- **Sichuan Opera bookings**: Popular shows at reputable venues sell out; book through AliTrip or hotel concierge at least 1 day ahead
