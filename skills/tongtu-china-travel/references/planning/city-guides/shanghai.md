# Shanghai

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2-4 (2 for highlights, 4 for depth + day trips)
- Best season: March–May (spring, mild and clear) and October–November (autumn, comfortable)
- Must-see: The Bund, Yu Garden, French Concession (Wukang Road + Tianzifang)
- Best area to stay: Huangpu or Jing'an — central, metro-accessible, walkable to Bund
- Key constraint: Most major museums require advance reservation; Yu Garden and Nanxiang Xiaolongbao have significant queues on weekends

## City Orientation

Shanghai is divided by the Huangpu River: Puxi (west, historic city) and Pudong (east, modern skyline). Most tourist activity is in Puxi; Pudong is best viewed from the west bank or visited briefly for the tower observation decks.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Huangpu (黄浦)** | The Bund, Yu Garden, People's Square, Shanghai Museum | Lines 1, 2, 10 |
| **French Concession (静安/徐汇)** | Xintiandi, Tianzifang, Wukang Road, Anfu Road cafes | Lines 1, 9, 10 |
| **Pudong (浦东)** | Shanghai Tower, Oriental Pearl, Lujiazui, Shanghai Museum East | Lines 2, 14 |
| **North Art District (普陀)** | M50 Art District, Suzhou Creek waterfront | Lines 3, 4, 13 |
| **Outside city (郊外)** | Zhujiajiao water town, Suzhou (高铁), Hangzhou (高铁) | Metro Line 17 / 高铁 from Hongqiao |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 20 lines covering all tourist zones. Alipay QR code for entry. ¥4-9 per ride.
- **Taxi/Didi**: Use Didi in Alipay. Avoid flagging taxis near the Bund and Yu Garden — unlicensed and tourist-pricing issues are common.
- **Walking**: Huangpu and French Concession zones are very walkable once you're in the zone. Crossing zones on foot is not recommended.
- **To Zhujiajiao**: Metro Line 17 to Zhujiajiao Station + 10-minute walk (~1.5h total from city center). Go on weekdays to avoid crowds.
- **To Suzhou/Hangzhou**: High-speed rail (G-train) from Hongqiao Station — Suzhou 30min, Hangzhou 45min.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### The Bund (外滩)
- **Time needed**: 30–60 min (walk), longer if lingering
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open 24 hours (outdoor promenade)
- **Hours**: Anytime; best before 08:00 (fewer crowds, good photography light) or after 20:00 (Pudong skyline fully lit)
- **Tips**: The Bund itself is the west bank promenade facing Pudong. For the best skyline photo, walk to the south end near Waibaidu Bridge or the north end near the old consulate buildings. Avoid midday — harsh light and peak crowds.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Yu Garden (豫园)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Recommended — book online the day before, especially in peak season (May, Oct). Tickets can sell out by mid-morning on weekends.
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 09:00–17:00 (last entry 16:30)
- **Tips**: A 16th-century classical garden — small but densely detailed. Go at opening to beat the tour groups. The bazaar (豫园商城) surrounding it is touristy but useful for snacks.
- **Ticket**: ¥40

#### The Bund Night View (外滩夜景)
- **Time needed**: 45 min
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open 24 hours
- **Hours**: Pudong skyline lights up around 19:00 and stays lit until midnight
- **Tips**: This is effectively a second visit to the Bund — the nighttime skyline is far more dramatic than daytime. Plan two Bund visits if possible: one morning, one evening.
- **Ticket**: Free

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### French Concession — Wukang Road (武康路)
- **Time needed**: 45–90 min (walk + coffee stop)
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily (outdoor street)
- **Hours**: Anytime; best 09:00–11:00 before it gets crowded
- **Tips**: The Wukang Mansion (武康大楼) at the south end of the street is the main landmark — a 1924 French-style building with a distinctive wedge shape. The surrounding streets (Anfu Road, Fuxing Road) have Shanghai's best independent cafe concentration.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Tianzifang (田子坊)
- **Time needed**: 1–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Shops generally 10:00–22:00
- **Tips**: A dense network of alleys converted from 1930s shikumen (stone-gate) residential lanes. More local and less polished than Xintiandi. Best for browsing independent shops, not speed sightseeing. Crowded after 11:00 on weekends.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Shanghai Museum — People's Square (上海博物馆)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Required — free online booking, release at 09:00 daily for next 7 days
- **Closed**: Open daily (new system; verify before visiting)
- **Hours**: 09:00–17:00 (last entry 16:00)
- **Tips**: Best collections: ancient bronzes (floor 1), ceramics (floor 2), calligraphy (floor 3). Do not attempt all floors in one visit. Free entry.
- **Ticket**: Free (with advance reservation)

#### Shanghai Tower Observation Deck (上海中心大厦)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours including queuing and viewing
- **Reservation**: Buy online to skip the ground-floor queue (saves 30–60 min)
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 10:00–21:30 (last elevator 21:00)
- **Tips**: 118th floor at 546m — the highest public observation deck in China. Best visited at dusk (17:00–19:00) to see the city transition from daylight to lit skyline. The Pudong towers look most impressive from across the river (the Bund) rather than from above.
- **Ticket**: ¥180 (standard) / ¥130 (advance online)

## Food & Dining

Shanghai specialties foreign tourists should try:

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Xiaolongbao (小笼包) | Soup dumplings; pork filling with hot broth inside | 南翔馒头店 (Yu Garden — queue expected); 佳家汤包 (local favorite, shorter queue) |
| Shengjian bao (生煎包) | Pan-fried buns — crispy bottom, juicy pork inside | 小杨生煎 (citywide); best eaten immediately |
| Scallion oil noodles (葱油拌面) | Cold noodles tossed in intensely fragrant scallion oil | Any local breakfast shop (早餐店) before 09:00 |
| Red-braised pork belly (本帮红烧肉) | Slow-braised in soy, sugar, and Shaoxing wine — sweet and savory | 老吉士 (book ahead), 德兴馆 |
| Hairy crab (大闸蟹) | Seasonal Sep–Nov only; steamed whole, eaten with ginger tea | Any decent restaurant in season; agree on price before ordering |
| Pan-fried pork chop with rice cake (排骨年糕) | Shanghai street classic | 云南南路 food street |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Bund/Yu Garden area**: 云南南路 (budget local food), 黄河路 (old-school Shanghai restaurants)
- **French Concession**: 巨鹿路 and 长乐路 (cafes and casual dining), 新天地 (upscale)
- **Late night**: 云南南路 stays open past 22:00

## Practical Tips for Shanghai

- **Rush hour**: Metro lines 1, 2, and 10 are extremely crowded 07:30–09:30 and 17:30–19:30. Avoid at these times if possible
- **Queue culture**: Unlike Beijing, Shanghai visitors queue seriously. Join the back — cutting does not work
- **Bund scams**: Men near the Bund may offer "art exhibitions" or "tea ceremonies" — these are tourist traps with inflated bills. Decline politely
- **Water**: Tap water is NOT drinkable. Bottled water is ¥2-3 everywhere
- **Card payments**: Alipay covers virtually all transactions including metro, Didi, and restaurants. Keep ¥200 cash as backup for small vendors
- **Photography**: Drone flying is prohibited over the Bund and Pudong without permits. Street photography is unrestricted

## Gotchas
- **Monday museum closures**: Shanghai Natural History Museum, Shanghai Science & Tech Museum, and Power Station of Art all close Monday — do not plan museum visits without checking
- **Xiaolongbao queue math**: The Yu Garden branch of 南翔馒头店 can have 60–90 minute waits on weekends. Either arrive at 09:00 sharp or go to 佳家汤包 in Jing'an
- **Pudong by day is underwhelming**: The towers look best from the Bund (west bank) at night. If you visit Shanghai Tower, go at dusk — the daytime view is hazy and unspectacular
- **Hairy crab pricing**: Outside Sep–Nov season, crab roe dishes (蟹粉) are made from frozen or imported roe — they exist but are significantly inferior. Check the season before ordering
- **Suzhou high-speed rail leaves from Hongqiao**: Not Shanghai Station (上海站). Hongqiao is 45min from the Bund by metro — factor in travel time
