# Nanjing

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2-3 (2 for highlights, 3 to add Purple Mountain depth or Yangzhou day trip)
- Best season: March–April (spring blossoms on Purple Mountain) and October–November (autumn foliage around the mausoleum); avoid July–August (Nanjing is one of China's "three furnaces" — extreme summer heat)
- Must-see: Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum, Confucius Temple (夫子庙) waterfront, Ming City Wall
- Best area to stay: Xinjiekou (新街口) — city center, metro hub, walkable to Confucius Temple; or 玄武湖 lakeside for a quieter option
- Key constraint: Purple Mountain (紫金山) sites are spread over a large forested area — the Mausoleum and Ming Tomb are several kilometers apart and not walkable between them. Use the scenic area bus or Didi.

## City Orientation

Nanjing was China's capital during the Ming dynasty and the Republic of China era (1912–1949). This layered history makes it one of China's most historically significant cities — Ming imperial sites, Republican-era architecture, and WWII history coexist. The Ming City Wall (still largely intact at 35.3km) encircles much of the historic city.

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 10 lines. Alipay QR code. ¥2-7 per ride. Lines 1 and 2 are the main tourist lines.
- **Purple Mountain scenic bus**: A free or low-cost shuttle runs between the main Purple Mountain sites (Mausoleum, Ming Tomb, Linggu Temple). Buy a day pass at the scenic area entrance (~¥30).
- **Didi**: Essential for Purple Mountain if not using the scenic bus. City-wide fares are reasonable.
- **To Shanghai**: 高铁 from 南京南站 to 上海虹桥 (~1h-1.5h) or 上海站. One of China's most convenient city pairs. Use `flyai search-train` for current schedules and prices.
- **To Yangzhou**: Bus from 南京汽车东站 (~1.5h); an easy day trip.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Sun Yat-sen Mausoleum (中山陵)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required — free entry; buy scenic area pass at the gate or online
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 06:30–18:00 (peak season) / 07:00–17:30 (off-peak)
- **Tips**: The marble mausoleum of Sun Yat-sen (孙中山), the founder of the Republic of China, built 1926–1929 in a forest of plane trees on Purple Mountain. 392 steps lead from the memorial archway to the burial chamber at the summit — each landing reveals a wider view of the forested city below. The climb takes 15–20 min. Arrive by 09:00 to avoid the heaviest crowds. The surrounding forests of the mountain are beautiful at any season; spring (cherry blossom) and autumn (foliage) are the peaks.
- **Ticket**: Scenic area pass ¥80 (covers Mausoleum, Ming Tomb, and Linggu Temple — buy at entrance)

#### Confucius Temple + Qinhuai River (夫子庙 / 秦淮河)
- **Time needed**: 2–2.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; riverfront best in the evening
- **Hours**: Temple grounds 08:00–21:00; waterfront always open
- **Tips**: The Qinhuai River district around the Confucius Temple (founded 1034 CE) is Nanjing's historic entertainment quarter — in the Ming dynasty, it was China's most famous literary and pleasure district. The river-side architecture has been restored; the best experience is renting a wooden boat (¥60-100/30min, booked at the riverside) and drifting past the lantern-lit historic buildings at night. Zhanyuan Garden (瞻园, a Ming private garden) is adjacent — one of the most beautiful gardens in eastern China.
- **Ticket**: Confucius Temple complex ¥30; Zhanyuan Garden ¥65; boat rental separate

#### Ming City Wall (明城墙)
- **Time needed**: 1–3 hours depending on section walked
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; specific gates and wall sections have varying hours
- **Hours**: Main sections 08:00–18:00; Zhonghua Gate (中华门) 08:30–18:30
- **Tips**: At 35.3km, this is the longest surviving city wall in the world. Zhonghua Gate (中华门) in the south is the most architecturally impressive section — a four-layered fortress gate with 27 hidden soldier caves. Walk the top of the Xuanwu Lake section for the best panoramic views. The wall uses different brick sizes from different counties and provinces (each brick is stamped with the origin county and supervisor name for quality control).
- **Ticket**: Zhonghua Gate ¥70; wall walk sections ¥20-35

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Ming Tomb (明孝陵) — UNESCO World Heritage
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required (included in Purple Mountain scenic pass)
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 06:30–18:00
- **Tips**: The tomb of the Hongwu Emperor, founder of the Ming dynasty (1368-1398). The Sacred Way (神道) is the highlight — a 1.8km processional avenue lined with 24 pairs of stone animals and officials, the most significant Ming-era sculptural ensemble in existence. The tomb mound itself has never been excavated. The surrounding wisteria forest is spectacular in April. Allow 30 min more than planned — the site is larger than maps suggest.
- **Ticket**: Included in Purple Mountain scenic pass ¥80

#### Nanjing Massacre Memorial (侵华日军南京大屠杀遇难同胞纪念馆)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Required — book free tickets online in advance; daily quota; must bring passport
- **Closed**: Monday (except national holidays)
- **Hours**: 08:30–17:00 (last entry 16:00)
- **Tips**: A solemn memorial to the 300,000 victims of the 1937 Nanjing Massacre. The exhibition is graphically documented with historical photographs, artifacts, survivor testimony, and archaeological evidence. This is not a comfortable visit — it is an important one. Maintain respectful behavior and dress. No photography in the main exhibition hall. Do not schedule this as the first item of a day — allow emotional space after the visit. The outdoor memorial garden and peace park are part of the complex.
- **Ticket**: Free (advance booking required)

#### Xuanwu Lake + Jiming Temple (玄武湖 / 鸡鸣寺)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours combined
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Lake park 05:00–21:00; Jiming Temple 07:00–17:30
- **Tips**: A large imperial-era lake within the old city walls. The lakeside path adjacent to the Ming City Wall is one of Nanjing's finest walks. The cherry blossoms at Jiming Temple in late March are among the most photographed in the city — arrive before 08:00 to avoid queues in blossom season.
- **Ticket**: Lake free; Jiming Temple ¥10

## Food & Dining

Nanjing cuisine (金陵菜) belongs to the Huaiyang tradition — refined, delicate, and focused on duck. Nanjing consumes more duck per capita than any city in China.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Salted duck (盐水鸭) | Cold poached duck cured in salt and star anise; eaten cold, sliced — the defining Nanjing dish | 桂花鸭 (the most famous brand, multiple locations); also vacuum-packed as a takeaway gift |
| Duck blood and vermicelli soup (鸭血粉丝汤) | Silky rice vermicelli in rich duck broth with duck blood tofu, duck organs, and tofu skin | Street stalls and restaurants near 夫子庙; ¥15-25 |
| Nanjing tangbao (南京汤包) | Soup dumplings in the Jiangnan style — slightly larger than Shanghai; light and delicate | 蒋有记 and restaurant chains near 新街口 |
| Osmanthus cake (桂花糕) | Sweet cake scented with Nanjing's famous osmanthus blossoms; best in September–October | Confucius Temple shops and bakeries |
| Duck fat sesame cake (鸭油烧饼) | Flaky sesame pastry made with rendered duck fat | Street stalls near 夫子庙 and 鸡鸣寺 |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Confucius Temple (夫子庙)**: Street food and traditional restaurants; 秦淮人家 restaurant complex for a sit-down meal
- **Xinjiekou / 湖南路**: More modern restaurants and local chains
- **老门东 (Old South Gate area)**: Restored historic district south of Zhonghua Gate with craft beer bars and updated Nanjing food

## Practical Tips for Nanjing

- **Nanjing Massacre Memorial**: Book the free ticket online before arriving in Nanjing — the quota fills up, especially on weekends and December 13 (National Memorial Day, when the site may be closed to general visitors). Bring your passport.
- **Summer heat**: Nanjing (along with Chongqing and Wuhan) is one of China's "三大火炉" (three furnaces). July–August temperatures regularly exceed 38°C with high humidity. Schedule outdoor activities before 10:00 and after 16:00.
- **Purple Mountain transport**: The sites are too spread out to walk between. Use the scenic area bus (频繁运行, included in the pass) or Didi between Mausoleum and Ming Tomb.
- **Salted duck as a gift**: Vacuum-packed 桂花鸭 salted duck is the most common Nanjing food souvenir. It keeps 3 days unrefrigerated and can be taken on the train. Ask for 真空包装 (vacuum packed) at any 桂花鸭 shop.
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable

## Gotchas
- **Nanjing Massacre Memorial on December 13**: National Memorial Day brings large crowds and formal ceremonies — the site may have restricted access for general visitors. Avoid if your dates coincide.
- **Purple Mountain scenic pass scope**: The ¥80 pass covers the main sites but some sub-attractions (specific sections of Linggu Temple) charge separately. Confirm at the gate what is included.
- **Jiming Temple cherry blossoms**: Late March blossom season draws enormous queues — arrive before 08:00 or use a ticketing platform to pre-book. The blossoms last only 5–7 days at peak.
- **Zhanyuan Garden (瞻园) is often overlooked**: This Ming-dynasty private garden adjacent to the Confucius Temple is genuinely beautiful and far less crowded than the main tourist drag. Seek it out.
- **Summer at the Mausoleum**: The 392-step climb to Sun Yat-sen's mausoleum in July–August heat is physically demanding. Carry water and start before 09:00.
