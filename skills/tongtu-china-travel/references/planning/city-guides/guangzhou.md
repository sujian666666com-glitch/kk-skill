# Guangzhou

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2-3 (2 for highlights, 3 for depth + Foshan day trip)
- Best season: October–March (cool, dry, and clear — the best season for outdoor sightseeing and hairy crab)
- Must-see: Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, a full 早茶 (dim sum) morning, Canton Tower at dusk
- Best area to stay: Tianhe (modern, metro hub) or Liwan (walkable to historic attractions)
- Key constraint: Famous dim sum restaurants require advance reservation on weekends; Canton Fair (April and October) causes city-wide hotel price spikes — avoid or book months ahead

## City Orientation

Guangzhou is less linear than Beijing or Shanghai — the historic zones (Liwan, Yuexiu) are west of the modern CBD (Tianhe). The Pearl River runs east-west through the city; the Canton Tower is on the south bank in Haizhu district. Most foreign tourists underestimate how far apart the zones are — plan one zone per half-day.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **荔湾 Liwan** | Chen Clan Ancestral Hall, Shamian Island, Enning Road, Shangxiajiu | Line 1 (陈家祠站), Line 6 (黄沙站) |
| **越秀 Yuexiu** | Yuexiu Park, Beijing Road, Nanyue King Museum, Guangzhou Museum | Lines 1, 2 (公园前站), Line 5 (小北站) |
| **海珠 Haizhu** | Canton Tower, Guangzhou Opera House, Pearl River cruise | Lines 3, 8 (广州塔站) |
| **天河 Tianhe** | Modern CBD, Zhujiang New Town, Flower City Square | Lines 1, 3, 5 (体育西路站) |
| **Outside city (郊外)** | Foshan Ancestral Temple, Shunde (顺德), Kaiping Diaolou | Metro or bus; 30min–2h |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: Lines 1, 2, 3, 5, 6 cover all tourist zones. Alipay QR code for entry. ¥2-9 per ride.
- **Taxi/Didi**: Use Didi in Alipay. English is less common among Guangzhou taxi drivers than in Shanghai or Beijing — use AliTrip's AI taxi feature to generate a Chinese-language driver card.
- **Walking**: Liwan and Yuexiu are walkable within zones. Cross-zone walking is not practical.
- **To Foshan**: Metro Line 2 / Guangfo Line (广佛线) — direct to 祖庙站, ~30min from city center.
- **To Shenzhen/Hong Kong**: High-speed rail from 广州南站 — Shenzhen 35min, Hong Kong West Kowloon 1hr.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Chen Clan Ancestral Hall (陈家祠)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required — buy at the gate or online
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:30–17:30 (last entry 17:00)
- **Tips**: The finest example of Lingnan folk architecture in China — 108 categories of decorative craft covering every surface (wood carving, stone carving, brick carving, ceramic ridge decorations, cast iron screens). Go slowly. Bring a zoom lens if you can — the upper-level friezes reward close inspection. A folk crafts museum inside shows the making process.
- **Ticket**: ¥10

#### 早茶 Dim Sum Morning (Morning Tea Experience)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Required for 广州酒家 and 泮溪酒家 on weekends — book 1–2 days ahead for the first morning slot (07:00–09:00). Weekday walk-ins are usually fine.
- **Closed**: Morning tea service is 07:00–11:00 ONLY — this is non-negotiable. Cannot be replicated at lunch or dinner.
- **Hours**: Plan at least one morning itinerary around this. It is Guangzhou's defining experience.
- **Tips**: Order from trolleys or a checksheet, not a menu. Four to six small dishes per person is typical. The 虾饺 (har gow) is the benchmark dish — a dim sum kitchen's skill is judged by it. Settle the bill per dish; the total is usually lower than expected.
- **Ticket**: ¥60–150/person depending on restaurant and dishes ordered

#### Canton Tower (广州塔)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours including queue
- **Reservation**: Buy observation deck tickets online in advance — saves 30–60 min of ground-floor queuing
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 10:00–22:30 (last elevator 22:00)
- **Tips**: Best visited at dusk (17:30–19:00) to catch the city in daylight then watch it light up. The external bubble tram (摩天轮 "Sky Drop") circles the outer ring — a unique experience but not essential. The tower is more visually striking from across the Pearl River (north bank, opposite side) than from the observation deck looking out.
- **Ticket**: ¥150 (main observation deck) / ¥200 (with Sky Drop)

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Shamian Island (沙面岛)
- **Time needed**: 45–60 min
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open 24 hours (outdoor)
- **Hours**: Best in late afternoon (15:00–18:00) for the light on the colonial buildings
- **Tips**: A 0.3km² former British and French colonial concession — tree-lined streets with European-style consulate buildings from the 1860s–1930s. Best as a leisurely walk rather than a destination in itself. Combine with Enning Road (10-minute walk east) for a half-day Liwan loop.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Enning Road / Yongqing Fang (恩宁路 / 永庆坊)
- **Time needed**: 1–1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; individual cafes and shops typically 10:00–22:00
- **Hours**: Best on weekday mornings; weekends get crowded after 11:00
- **Tips**: A sensitively restored Qing-era neighborhood — the best-executed historic renovation in Guangzhou. Original residential buildings now house independent cafes, bookshops, and studios without losing the lane structure. Walk north from the main Yongqing Fang entrance into the quieter residential alleys for a less curated experience.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Nanyue King Museum (西汉南越王博物馆)
- **Time needed**: 1–1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Monday
- **Hours**: 09:00–17:30 (last entry 17:00)
- **Tips**: One of the most significant archaeological finds in southern China — built over the actual 2nd-century BC tomb of the second Nanyue King, discovered intact in 1983. The burial suit made of jade discs sewn with silk thread is extraordinary. The new museum wing opened 2023 has significantly improved the exhibition design.
- **Ticket**: ¥15

#### Yuexiu Park + Guangzhou Museum (越秀公园 / 广州博物馆)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours combined
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Museum closed Monday; park open daily
- **Hours**: Park 06:00–21:00; Zhenhai Tower Museum 09:00–17:30
- **Tips**: Go before 08:00 to experience the park as locals do — tai chi, fan dancing, group singing, and badminton fill the paths and terraces. The Zhenhai Tower (镇海楼) is a five-story Ming fortress containing the city history museum, with the city wall remnants visible in the park grounds.
- **Ticket**: Park free; Zhenhai Tower ¥15

## Food & Dining

> Guangzhou is China's most significant food city. Cantonese cuisine defines what most of the world understands as "Chinese food." Give food more weight in Guangzhou than in any other city on the itinerary.

### 早茶 Dim Sum — What to Order

The 虾饺 (har gow) is the benchmark dish — a dim sum kitchen's technique is judged by the thinness and translucency of the wrapper.

| Dish | Chinese | Description |
|------|---------|-------------|
| Steamed shrimp dumpling | 虾饺 | The benchmark dish; thin translucent wrapper, whole shrimp inside |
| Open pork dumpling | 烧卖 | Pork and shrimp, open-topped; firm texture |
| Rice noodle roll | 肠粉 | Silky steamed rice sheet, filled with shrimp or BBQ pork; order 虾肠 or 叉烧肠 |
| Pan-fried turnip cake | 萝卜糕 | Crispy outside, soft inside; savory flavor |
| BBQ pork bun | 叉烧包 | Baked (焗) or steamed (蒸); both worth trying |
| Egg tart | 蛋挞 | Cantonese flaky pastry version; entirely different from the Portuguese-style |
| Lotus leaf sticky rice | 糯米鸡 | Glutinous rice with chicken, mushroom, and lap cheong in a lotus leaf |

### Cantonese BBQ (烧腊) — The Street-Level Standard

Available from dedicated 烧腊店 from 11:00 onward, eaten over rice.

| Item | Chinese | Note |
|------|---------|------|
| Roast goose | 烧鹅 | Guangzhou's signature; 深井烧鹅 style — crackling skin, juicy meat. Order at 裕记深井烧鹅 in Liwan |
| Char siu | 叉烧 | Honey-glazed BBQ pork; order 五花叉烧 (fatty cut) for best texture |
| White cut chicken | 白切鸡 | Poached in clear stock, served cold with ginger-scallion oil; the purest Cantonese cooking |
| Crispy pork belly | 脆皮烧肉 | Must be audibly crispy when served |

### Other Must-try Dishes

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Rice noodle rolls (肠粉) | Softer, thicker than the dim sum version; steamed fresh from the steamer, served with soy, sesame, and peanut sauce | Dedicated 拉肠店; best before 10:00 |
| Wonton noodles (云吞面) | Thin lye-water egg noodles with pork-shrimp wontons in clear broth; noodles must have snap (弹牙) | 池记云吞面 (most famous) |
| Clay pot rice (煲仔饭) | Rice steamed over charcoal in a clay pot; the scorched crust (锅巴) at the bottom is the prize | Liwan area restaurants, from 11:00 |
| Bamboo-pressed noodles (竹升面) | Dense springy egg noodles pressed with a bamboo pole; usually in wonton soup | 麦奀记 (classic reference) |
| Cantonese sweet soup (糖水) | Dessert soups: 双皮奶 (milk pudding), 芝麻糊 (sesame paste), 姜撞奶 (ginger milk curd) | Specialist 糖水铺; 满记甜品 chain is reliable |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Liwan (morning/lunch)**: 泮塘 near Liwan Lake for traditional dim sum; 上下九 for snacks and old brands; 恩宁路 for cafes
- **Yuexiu (lunch)**: Local noodle shops and 烧腊 shops near 北京路 and 越秀公园
- **Haizhu / Huangsha (dinner)**: 黄沙水产市场 — buy live seafood at the market, have a restaurant nearby cook it for a preparation fee (~¥30-50); arrive by 11:00 for best selection

## Practical Tips for Guangzhou

- **English is less common**: Fewer English menus and English-speaking staff than in Shanghai or Beijing. Use AliTrip's camera translation feature for menus and signs — it is especially useful here
- **Cantonese vs Mandarin**: Locals primarily speak Cantonese; Mandarin is understood but Cantonese is appreciated. Basic Cantonese greetings: 你好 (nei hou — hello), 唔該 (m̀h gōi — thank you/excuse me)
- **Seafood pricing**: At 黄沙 seafood market, always confirm the price per jin (斤, ~500g) before purchase. Ask: 多少钱一斤? Final bills can be higher than expected if weight is not agreed upfront
- **Dim sum timing**: Morning tea ends at 11:00 sharp in most restaurants. Do not arrive after 10:30 expecting full service
- **Canton Fair avoidance**: Check 广交会 dates before booking. April Phase 1 (April 15–19) and Phase 2 (April 23–27) and October (October 15–19, 23–27) drive hotel prices 2–3× higher citywide
- **Water**: Tap water is NOT drinkable. Bottled water is ¥2-3 everywhere
- **Cash backup**: Alipay covers most transactions, but smaller 烧腊 shops and street stalls may be cash-only

## Gotchas
- **Dim sum window is narrow**: Morning tea is 07:00–11:00 only. This is not flexible — if you miss the morning, you cannot replicate the experience at lunch. Plan at least one full morning around it
- **Canton Fair doubles prices**: Many travelers are caught off-guard by the April and October price spikes. Even travelers not attending the fair are affected — all hotels citywide raise rates
- **Roast goose sells out**: The best 烧腊 shops sell out of roast goose (烧鹅) by early afternoon. Go before 13:00 or call ahead
- **Canton Tower is better from outside**: Most visitors are underwhelmed by the observation deck view. The tower looks far more spectacular from the north bank of the Pearl River at night — consider enjoying it from the ground rather than paying for the elevator
- **Historic zones close early**: 陈家祠 last entry 17:00; 南越王博物馆 last entry 17:00. Do not plan these as late-afternoon attractions
- **Monday closures cluster in Yuexiu**: 南越王博物馆, 广东省博物馆, and 广州博物馆 all close Monday. If your itinerary puts Yuexiu on Monday, there is almost nothing to do there
