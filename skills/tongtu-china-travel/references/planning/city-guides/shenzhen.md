# Shenzhen

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 1-2 (easily combined with Guangzhou or Hong Kong)
- Best season: October–March (cool and dry; avoid June–September typhoon season and extreme humidity)
- Must-see: OCT Loft creative district, Dafen Oil Painting Village, Sea World waterfront
- Best area to stay: Futian (central, metro hub) or Nanshan (near OCT and tech campuses)
- Key constraint: No single landmark attraction — Shenzhen's value is its energy, design culture, and proximity to Hong Kong. One focused day is usually enough for most foreign tourists

## City Orientation

Shenzhen is China's youngest major city (established 1980) and the world's fastest urban growth story. It lacks ancient sites but excels in design, technology, and contemporary culture. The city is long and narrow east-west, divided into districts along the metro lines.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **南山 Nanshan** | OCT Loft, Sea World, Shenzhen Bay Park, tech campuses | Lines 1, 2, 5 (华侨城站, 海上世界站) |
| **罗湖 Luohu** | Old border district, Dongmen shopping, Luohu Commercial City | Lines 1, 3 (罗湖站) |
| **福田 Futian** | CBD, Shenzhen Museum, Lianhuashan Park, Convention Center | Lines 1, 2, 3, 4, 11 (会展中心站) |
| **龙华 Longhua** | Dafen Oil Painting Village | Line 6 (大芬站) |
| **盐田 Yantian** | Dapeng Fortress, coastal hiking | Line 8 (沙头角站, then bus) |
| **到香港 To Hong Kong** | 30min by 高铁 to West Kowloon; 30min by metro to Lo Wu border | 高铁 from 福田站 or metro Line 1 to Luohu |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 16 lines covering the entire city. Alipay QR code at all gates. ¥2-9 per ride. Shenzhen's metro is fast and reliable.
- **Didi**: Works seamlessly; Shenzhen drivers typically have better English than in most Chinese cities.
- **Walking**: OCT Loft and Sea World are walkable between each other (~20min). Other zones require metro.
- **To Hong Kong**: 高铁 from 福田站 to Hong Kong West Kowloon — ~20min. Use `flyai search-train` for current schedules and prices. Or metro Line 1 to 罗湖 border crossing (slower, more pedestrian traffic).

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### OCT Loft Creative District (华侨城创意文化园)
- **Time needed**: 2–3 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Individual galleries and cafes closed Monday; outdoor spaces open daily
- **Hours**: Outdoor spaces always open; galleries typically 10:00–18:00
- **Tips**: A former industrial factory complex converted to Shenzhen's most successful creative district — design studios, galleries, cafes, and concept stores in red-brick warehouse buildings. The north zone (北区) is denser and more interesting than the south zone. Visit on a weekday to see studios in operation. Free to wander; individual gallery admission varies.
- **Ticket**: Free (outdoor); galleries ¥0–30

#### Dafen Oil Painting Village (大芬油画村)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; most studios 09:00–20:00
- **Hours**: 09:00–20:00 (most days)
- **Tips**: The world's largest oil painting production hub — hundreds of artists produce and sell originals and reproductions. You can commission a portrait or custom painting (allow 1–3 days for completion; shipping available). Prices range from ¥50 for a small reproduction to ¥5,000+ for large originals. Bargaining is expected. The village also has a free museum (大芬美术馆) showing local artists' work.
- **Ticket**: Free (village); Museum free

#### Sea World (海上世界)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Outdoor plaza 24 hours; shops and restaurants 10:00–22:00
- **Tips**: A waterfront development centered on a permanently docked 1973 French ocean liner (明华轮 MV Minghua). The liner itself is now a hotel and restaurant. The surrounding plaza has Shenzhen's best concentration of independent restaurants and bars. Best visited in the evening. Not a theme park despite the name.
- **Ticket**: Free (plaza)

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Shenzhen Museum (深圳博物馆)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required — but may require ID registration at gate
- **Closed**: Monday
- **Hours**: 10:00–18:00 (last entry 17:30)
- **Tips**: Compact, well-designed museum covering Shenzhen's extraordinary transformation from a fishing village to a city of 17 million in 40 years. The modern history section is genuinely fascinating — before/after photographs of the same locations across decades. Free entry.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Lianhuashan Park (莲花山公园)
- **Time needed**: 1 hour
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 06:00–23:00
- **Tips**: A large urban park with a hilltop statue of Deng Xiaoping. The summit gives a good panoramic view of Futian's CBD. Popular with locals in the morning. The park is pleasant rather than spectacular — worth combining with the nearby Shenzhen Museum.
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Shenzhen Bay Park (深圳湾公园)
- **Time needed**: 1 hour (walk)
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 06:00–23:00
- **Tips**: A 15km coastal greenway along Shenzhen Bay facing Hong Kong's New Territories across the water. Best at sunset for the Hong Kong skyline silhouette. Popular with cyclists and joggers. Bike rentals available along the path (Alipay-enabled shared bikes, ~¥3/30min).
- **Ticket**: Free

### Tier 3: If you have more time

| Attraction | Time needed | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Dapeng Fortress (大鹏所城) | Half day | 600-year-old Ming-dynasty coastal fortress; well-preserved. Far east of the city — 1.5h by metro + bus |
| Window of the World (世界之窗) | 2–3 hours | Scale replicas of global landmarks (Eiffel Tower, Taj Mahal, etc.). Kitschy but self-aware; best for families |
| Shenzhen's tech campuses | 1 hour | Huawei, Tencent, and DJI campuses can be viewed from outside; some have public visitor areas — check current policies |

## Food & Dining

Shenzhen does not have a strong local cuisine tradition — it is a city of migrants from across China, which makes it exceptionally diverse. Cantonese food dominates but every regional Chinese cuisine is available.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Cantonese dim sum (早茶) | See Guangzhou guide — same quality in Shenzhen | Upscale hotel restaurants; 皇庭广场 area |
| Sichuan hotpot (四川火锅) | Spicy broth with meat and vegetables; table cooking | Chains citywide: 海底捞 (English menu), 凑凑 |
| Fresh seafood (海鲜) | Grilled, steamed, or wok-fried fresh seafood | 沙头角 area (eastern Shenzhen, near the old border); also 蛇口渔港 near Sea World |
| Chaoshan beef hotpot (潮汕牛肉火锅) | Ultra-fresh beef sliced tableside, clean broth | Dedicated 潮汕牛肉火锅 restaurants citywide |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **OCT Loft area**: Best independent cafes and design-led restaurants in the city
- **Sea World**: Wide variety, bar scene, international options
- **罗湖 Dongmen**: Local Cantonese restaurants, budget noodle shops
- **福田 CBD**: International hotel restaurants, upscale options

## Practical Tips for Shenzhen

- **Most cashless city in China**: Alipay penetration is near-total — from street food to malls to taxis. This is also the easiest city in China to navigate without Chinese language skills
- **Young city**: Almost no one in Shenzhen is originally from Shenzhen. English proficiency is higher than average among the under-40 population
- **Hong Kong visa check**: Foreign nationals should verify whether their China visa allows re-entry before crossing to Hong Kong, as Hong Kong is a separate entry point
- **Shenzhen does not have a Forbidden City or Terracotta Warriors**: Visitors expecting ancient sites will be disappointed. The city's draw is modernity, design, and urban energy
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable. Bottled water ¥2-3 everywhere

## Gotchas
- **Shenzhen's sites close early in the evening**: OCT Loft galleries close at 18:00; plan accordingly
- **Dafen commission timing**: If commissioning a custom painting, you need 1–3 days — plan this at the start of your stay, not the end
- **Luohu border crossing crowds**: The road/metro border crossing to Hong Kong (罗湖) can have 1–2 hour queues on Friday afternoons and weekends. Use the 高铁 from 福田站 to Hong Kong instead — much faster
- **Window of the World is not what it sounds like**: It is a theme park with scale models, not an arts venue — adjust expectations accordingly
