# Chongqing

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2-3 (2 for city highlights, 3 to add Wulong day trip)
- Best season: September–November (cooler, clearer; summer is extremely hot and foggy; spring is also acceptable)
- Must-see: Hongyadong at night, Ciqikou Ancient Town, a full Chongqing hotpot meal
- Best area to stay: Yuzhong Peninsula (解放碑/Jiefangbei area) — walkable to Hongyadong, central location
- Key constraint: The city is extremely hilly — steep staircases and dramatic elevation changes are unavoidable. Comfortable shoes are essential. Wulong (武隆) requires advance booking and a full day.

## City Orientation

Chongqing sits at the confluence of the Yangtze (长江) and Jialing (嘉陵江) rivers. The city is built into vertical terrain — bridges, elevated highways, and metro lines run at different levels above the street. The famous "8D city" aesthetic (multi-level urban fabric) is not an exaggeration. Navigation is unlike any other Chinese city.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **渝中半岛 Yuzhong** | Jiefangbei, Hongyadong, Chaotianmen (river confluence), Liberation Monument | Lines 1, 2, 6 (两路口站, 临江门站) |
| **沙坪坝 Shapingba** | Ciqikou Ancient Town, universities | Line 1 (磁器口站) |
| **南岸 Nan'an** | Nanshan Observation Deck, Eling Park, south bank river views | Line 3 (南坪站) then bus |
| **洪崖洞 Hongyadong** | (Within Yuzhong) Stacked cliff-side restaurant/bar complex | Walk from 临江门站 |
| **郊外 Outside city** | Wulong Karst (武隆天坑), Three Gorges (三峡) | Bus 3hr / boat |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 12 lines. Alipay QR code works at all gates. ¥2-8 per ride. Note: Line 2 famously passes through a residential building at 李子坝站 — worth riding for the experience.
- **Monorail (Line 2)**: Best viewed from outside at 李子坝站 to see trains passing through the apartment building.
- **Cable car (索道)**: Two Yangtze River cable cars cross between Yuzhong and the south bank — a unique and practical way to cross. ¥5–10 per trip.
- **Walking**: Extremely hilly — what appears to be a short distance on a map may involve climbing 100+ steps. Use the metro for anything more than 500m.
- **Didi**: Essential for getting between zones; drivers navigate the complex road system better than any map.
- **To Wulong**: Long-distance bus from 龙头寺客运站 (~3h, ¥80-100) or booked tour car. Reserve Wulong scenic area tickets online.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Hongyadong (洪崖洞)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Shops and restaurants 10:00–23:00; the exterior cliff-face structure is lit until midnight
- **Tips**: An 11-story stacked complex of traditional Chongqing stilted buildings (吊脚楼) built into a 75m cliff face above the Jialing River. Best experienced at night when the illuminated tiers reflect in the river below — it resembles the bathhouse from Spirited Away, which is not a coincidence (Miyazaki's inspiration). Day visits are pleasant but far less dramatic. Walk up through the levels; the rooftop view of the Yangtze-Jialing confluence is worth the climb.
- **Ticket**: Free (exterior and walkways); restaurants and shops charge separately

#### Ciqikou Ancient Town (磁器口古镇)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 09:00–22:00
- **Tips**: A preserved Ming-dynasty riverside town with stone-paved streets and wooden shophouses. More authentic than Shanghai's Tianzifang — the core lanes away from the main street have residents still living there. Best before 10:00 or after 17:00 when day-tripper crowds thin. Specialties to try: 麻花 (twisted fried dough), 陈麻花, and 毛血旺 (a Chongqing staple).
- **Ticket**: Free

#### Yangtze River Confluence View (朝天门)
- **Time needed**: 45 min
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily (plaza)
- **Hours**: Anytime; best at dawn or dusk
- **Tips**: The nose of the Yuzhong Peninsula where the brown Jialing River meets the greener Yangtze. The color contrast between the two rivers is visible for weeks after their meeting. Chaotianmen Square was recently redeveloped — the Raffles City complex (重庆来福士) with its sky bridge connecting twin towers is adjacent and worth seeing from below.
- **Ticket**: Free

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Line 2 Through the Building (李子坝站)
- **Time needed**: 30 min
- **Reservation**: Not required — just ride the metro
- **Closed**: Operating hours of Metro Line 2
- **Hours**: 06:30–23:00 (metro operating hours)
- **Tips**: Stand at street level below 李子坝站 and watch the metro train pass through the 6th–8th floors of a residential apartment building — the train runs on a standard metro line that happens to pass through the structure. One of Chongqing's most photographed images. Alternatively, ride Line 2 through the station for the interior experience.
- **Ticket**: ¥2-4 (metro fare)

#### Nanshan Observation Deck (南山一棵树观景台)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours including transit
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 09:00–22:00 (best after 19:00 for night city panorama)
- **Tips**: The best panoramic view of Chongqing's dramatic skyline — the city in a river bend, towers rising from the hillsides, the two rivers visible below. Best at night. Take Line 3 to 南坪站 then a Didi (~20min). The view from here gives context for the city's extreme topography.
- **Ticket**: ¥15

#### Eling Park (鹅岭公园 / 两江楼)
- **Time needed**: 1 hour
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–22:00
- **Tips**: A public park on a ridge above Yuzhong with good views of both the Yangtze and Jialing rivers. Contains the 两江楼 (Two Rivers Pavilion) built in Republican-era style. Far less crowded than Nanshan and accessible on foot from the Jiefangbei area.
- **Ticket**: Free

### Tier 3: If you have more time

| Attraction | Time needed | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Wulong Karst (武隆天坑地缝) | Full day | UNESCO; dramatic limestone sinkholes and sky bridge. 3hr bus each way — requires full-day commitment. Book tickets online. Best April–October |

## Food & Dining

Chongqing is one of China's great food cities, defined by intense spice (麻辣 — numbing heat from Sichuan peppercorns + chili).

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Chongqing hotpot (重庆火锅) | The defining dish — boiling spicy broth with tripe, beef, vegetables, tofu. Order 鸳鸯锅 (split pot) for a mild half if needed | 老灶火锅, 德庄, 秦妈; also countless local shops near Jiefangbei |
| Chongqing noodles (重庆小面) | Thin noodles in spiced broth with chili oil, peanuts, and scallion; Chongqing's breakfast staple | Any local noodle shop; ¥8-15 |
| Spicy boiled offal (毛血旺) | Boiled blood curd, tripe, and vegetables in a fiery red oil broth | Ciqikou and local restaurants |
| Grilled fish (烤鱼) | Whole fish grilled then simmered in spicy toppings | Specialist 烤鱼 restaurants; 万州烤鱼 style is famous |
| Sour and spicy glass noodles (酸辣粉) | Potato starch noodles in a sour-spicy soup; street food classic | Street stalls and small restaurants citywide |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Jiefangbei area**: Dense concentration of hotpot restaurants; touristy but convenient
- **Ciqikou**: 毛血旺, 豆腐, and sweet snacks best eaten here on-site
- **Guanyinqiao (观音桥)**: Local dining area, less touristy, good for 小面 at breakfast

## Practical Tips for Chongqing

- **Shoes matter enormously**: The city requires constant stair climbing. Flip-flops and dress shoes are a serious mistake. Bring the most comfortable walking shoes you own
- **Hotpot heat levels**: 重庆火锅 is significantly hotter than Beijing or Shanghai hotpot. The default spice level is very high. Order 微辣 (mildly spicy) if you're cautious, or 鸳鸯锅 (split pot — half spicy, half clear) to control heat
- **Navigation apps struggle**: Chongqing's multi-level road system confuses standard navigation — routes that appear to be 200m may require going up 10 floors and back down. Trust Didi drivers to find the right entrance
- **Fog and visibility**: Chongqing's nickname is 雾都 (Fog City) — low clouds and mist are common in winter and spring. Clear days are rarer than in other Chinese cities. Adjust expectations for views
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable. Bottled water ¥2-3 everywhere

## Gotchas
- **Hongyadong by day is flat**: The cliff-face structure looks ordinary in daylight. Do not visit before 19:30 — the entire experience depends on the night illumination
- **Wulong requires a full day**: The bus from Chongqing takes 3 hours each way. Do not attempt Wulong as a half-day trip; it will leave no time at the site
- **Chongqing hotpot is not Sichuan hotpot**: Chongqing hotpot is spicier, richer, and uses more offal than the Sichuan (Chengdu) version. First-timers should order 鸳鸯锅
- **Cable car lines**: The Yangtze River cable car (长江索道) has long queues on weekends — arrive before 09:00 or after 16:00
- **Summer is brutal**: July and August temperatures regularly exceed 38°C with high humidity. Chongqing is one of China's "three furnaces." Visit in autumn if possible
