# Beijing

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 3-5 (3 for highlights, 5 for depth)
- Best season: September - November (clear skies, autumn foliage, comfortable temperatures)
- Must-see: Forbidden City, Great Wall, Temple of Heaven
- Best area to stay: Dongcheng District (walkable to Forbidden City, metro-accessible)
- Key constraint: Forbidden City + most museums require advance reservation and close Mondays

## City Orientation

Beijing is structured around a clear north-south central axis. The city is large but the main tourist zone is concentrated.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **Central axis (中轴线)** | Tiananmen Square, Forbidden City, Jingshan Park, Drum Tower | Line 1, Line 2 |
| **East of center (东城)** | Wangfujing, National Museum, Nanluoguxiang hutongs | Line 1, Line 5, Line 6 |
| **South (南城)** | Temple of Heaven, Qianmen, Dashilar old street | Line 5, Line 8 |
| **Northwest (西北)** | Summer Palace, Old Summer Palace, Peking University | Line 4, Line 16 |
| **Northeast (东北)** | 798 Art District, Olympic Park (Bird's Nest) | Line 14, Line 8 |
| **Outside city (郊外)** | Great Wall (Badaling / Mutianyu), Ming Tombs | Bus or tour; 1.5-2h one way |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: Covers all major tourist zones. Alipay QR code for entry. ¥3-7 per ride.
- **Taxi/Didi**: Reasonable (¥20-50 within tourist areas). Use Didi in Alipay.
- **Walking**: Central axis zone is very walkable once you're there.
- **To Great Wall**: No metro. Use Badaling Express bus from Deshengmen (¥12, ~80min) or book a car via Didi to Mutianyu (~¥200-300 each way, ~1.5-2h).

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Forbidden City (故宫博物院)
- **Time needed**: 3-4 hours (minimum 2.5h fast walk)
- **Reservation**: **MANDATORY** — book 7 days ahead via WeChat mini-program "故宫博物院" or official website (English available)
- **Closed**: Monday (except national holidays)
- **Hours**: 08:30 - 17:00 (last entry 16:10 summer / 15:40 winter)
- **Entry**: South gate (Meridian Gate / 午门) only; exit from north gate (Gate of Divine Might / 神武门)
- **Tips**: Passport required at entry. Audio guide available in English (¥20 deposit). Go early — 08:30 opening is least crowded.
- **Ticket**: ¥60 (Apr-Oct) / ¥40 (Nov-Mar)

#### Great Wall
Two main options for foreign tourists:

| | Badaling (八达岭) | Mutianyu (慕田峪) |
|---|---|---|
| Distance from city | 70km (~1.5h by bus) | 75km (~1.5-2h by car) |
| Crowds | **Very crowded** (most popular) | **Much less crowded** (recommended) |
| Facilities | Cable car, easy access, fully restored | Cable car + toboggan down, partially wild |
| Difficulty | Easy — gentle slopes, wide paths | Moderate — steeper in sections |
| Best for | Families, limited mobility | Photographers, atmosphere-seekers, most foreign tourists |
| Getting there | Bus 877 from Deshengmen (¥12) or Badaling Express train | Didi/tour car (¥200-300) or bus from Dongzhimen |
| Reservation | Required — WeChat mini-program | Required — mini-program or on-site |

**Recommendation for foreign tourists: Mutianyu.** Fewer crowds, more authentic feel, toboggan ride down is fun. Allocate a full day.

#### Temple of Heaven (天坛)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Reservation**: Required for the inner hall area; park grounds are open
- **Closed**: Monday (inner halls only; park open daily)
- **Hours**: Park 06:00-21:00; inner halls 08:00-17:00
- **Tips**: Go before 09:00 to see locals doing tai chi, dancing, and singing in the park. This is a genuine cultural experience.
- **Ticket**: ¥15 (park only) / ¥34 (combined with inner halls)

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Summer Palace (颐和园)
- **Time needed**: 3-4 hours
- **Hours**: 06:30-18:00 (summer) / 07:00-17:00 (winter)
- **Tips**: Take a boat on Kunming Lake (¥10-15). The Long Corridor (长廊) has 14,000 painted scenes. Combine with Old Summer Palace if you have time.
- **Ticket**: ¥30 (low season) / ¥60 (combined ticket with all halls)

#### Tiananmen Square + National Museum
- **Time needed**: Tiananmen Square 30-60 min (outdoor); National Museum 2-3 hours
- **Reservation**: National Museum requires free advance booking (WeChat mini-program)
- **Closed**: National Museum closed Monday
- **Tips**: Security check to enter the square. Bag storage available at National Museum. The museum is enormous — pick 1-2 exhibition halls rather than trying to see everything.

#### Hutong neighborhoods (胡同)
- **Best areas**: Nanluoguxiang (南锣鼓巷), Wudaoying (五道营), Shichahai (什刹海) / Houhai area
- **Time needed**: 1.5-3 hours wandering
- **Tips**: Nanluoguxiang is the most touristy but easiest to find. For a more authentic experience, walk the smaller alleys east/west of Nanluoguxiang. Rickshaw tours available (negotiate ¥100-150/30min).
- **No reservation needed**

#### 798 Art District (798艺术区)
- **Time needed**: 2-3 hours
- **Hours**: Galleries generally 10:00-18:00; some closed Monday
- **Tips**: Free to enter the district; individual galleries may charge. Great for photography. Combine with lunch at one of the cafes inside.

### Tier 3: If you have more time

| Attraction | Time | Notes |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Jingshan Park (景山公园) | 1 hour | Best panoramic view of Forbidden City from the hilltop; directly north of the exit |
| Beihai Park (北海公园) | 1.5 hours | Beautiful imperial garden with white pagoda; adjacent to Forbidden City area |
| Lama Temple (雍和宫) | 1-1.5 hours | Active Tibetan Buddhist temple; stunning architecture; Line 2/5 Yonghegong station |
| Olympic Park | 1 hour | Photo op with Bird's Nest and Water Cube; more impressive at night with lights |
| Ming Tombs (明十三陵) | Half day | Often combined with Badaling Great Wall day trip |

## Food & Dining Areas

Beijing specialties foreign tourists should try:

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Peking Duck (北京烤鸭) | Crispy-skin roast duck with pancakes | Quanjude, Dadong, Siji Minfu (四季民福 — local favorite) |
| Zhajiangmian (炸酱面) | Thick noodles with soybean paste | Any local noodle shop; Lao Beijing Zhajiangmian chains |
| Hotpot (火锅) | Cook-your-own meat/veggies in boiling broth | Haidilao (海底捞 — English menu, excellent service) |
| Jianbing (煎饼) | Savory breakfast crepe from street vendors | Street stalls in the morning; ¥8-15 |
| Baozi (包子) | Steamed stuffed buns | Qingfeng (庆丰包子铺) — Xi Jinping once ate here, now famous |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Forbidden City area**: Jingshan West Street, Beihai area
- **Hutong area**: Nanluoguxiang (touristy), Gulou East Street (more local)
- **Modern/upscale**: Sanlitun, CBD area
- **Night food**: Guijie (簋街 "Ghost Street") — seafood and spicy food street, open late

## Practical Tips for Beijing

- **Air quality**: Check AQI before outdoor plans. If AQI > 150, consider indoor alternatives (National Museum, malls). Apps: use the built-in weather app or search "Beijing air quality" online
- **Forbidden City scale**: It's 72 hectares (178 acres). You WILL walk 15,000+ steps. Wear comfortable shoes, bring water
- **Security checks**: Bags are X-rayed at metro stations, Tiananmen Square, and major attractions. Pocket knives and large liquids are confiscated
- **Cash backup**: Most places accept Alipay/WeChat, but the Great Wall vendors and some hutong shops may prefer cash
- **Taxi tips**: Use Didi (via Alipay) — street hails are harder and unlicensed drivers may overcharge foreigners. Didi has English UI
- **Water**: Tap water is NOT drinkable. Buy bottled water (¥2-3 everywhere) or use hotel kettle
- **Photography**: No flash in museums. Drone flying is banned over central Beijing (military airspace). Street photography is fine.

## Gotchas
- **Monday curse**: Forbidden City, National Museum, Capital Museum, and most museums close Monday. Do NOT plan a museum day on Monday
- **Forbidden City sells out**: During peak season (May, Oct), tickets sell out instantly when released 7 days ahead. Set an alarm for the release time (usually 20:00 seven days prior)
- **Great Wall weather**: It's windier and 3-5°C colder on the wall than in the city. Bring an extra layer even in summer
- **Tiananmen flag ceremony**: Flag raising at sunrise, lowering at sunset — draws big crowds. Interesting to watch but plan around it, not to it
- **Smog season**: December-February can have persistent haze. Blue sky days are unpredictable. If air quality matters to you, visit in autumn (Sep-Nov)
