# Xi'an

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 3-4 (3 for highlights, 4 to add Huashan or Famen Temple)
- Best season: April–May and September–October (comfortable temperatures, clear skies; avoid summer heat and the spring sandstorm window in March)
- Must-see: Terracotta Warriors (the non-negotiable), Muslim Quarter (回民街), City Wall bike ride
- Best area to stay: Inside the city walls (城内) — walkable to Muslim Quarter, Bell Tower, and Drum Tower; convenient for metro to Terracotta Warriors
- Key constraint: Terracotta Warriors site is 40km from the city center — allow a full morning and part of the afternoon. Book tickets online. Advance reservation is required during peak season.

## City Orientation

Xi'an (西安) is one of China's four ancient capitals and the eastern terminus of the Silk Road. It was the capital of 13 dynasties, most significantly the Han and Tang. The old city is still enclosed within a complete 14km Ming-dynasty city wall — the most intact example in China. The Terracotta Warriors are not in the city — they are a 40km, 1-hour journey to the east.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **城内 Inside City Walls** | Bell Tower, Drum Tower, Muslim Quarter, Great Mosque, Shuyuanmen | Lines 2, 3 (钟楼站, 南门站) |
| **雁塔 Yanta** | Big Wild Goose Pagoda, Shaanxi History Museum, Tang Paradise | Lines 3, 4 (大雁塔站, 小寨站) |
| **城墙 City Wall** | 14km wall circuit (bike rentals at multiple gates) | Walk from any city gate |
| **临潼 Lintong** (40km east) | Terracotta Warriors, Huaqing Palace (华清宫) | Metro Line 9 to 华清路站 (~1h) or bus |
| **郊外 Outside city** | Huashan Mountain (华山), Famen Temple | High-speed rail or bus; 1.5–2h |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 6 lines. Alipay QR code. ¥2-7 per ride. Line 9 is critical — it runs east toward the Terracotta Warriors.
- **To Terracotta Warriors**: Metro Line 9 to 华清路站, then tourist bus or Didi (~20min) to the site. Total: ~1h from Bell Tower. Alternatively, Didi directly (¥80-100 each way).
- **City Wall**: The wall itself is the transport — rent a bike at any of the main gates (南门 recommended) and cycle the 14km circuit. ¥45 for 100 min.
- **Didi**: Works well throughout the city and to nearby sites.
- **To Huashan**: 高铁 from 西安北站 to 华山北站 (~35min), then cable car or hiking path up.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Terracotta Warriors (秦始皇兵马俑博物馆) — UNESCO World Heritage
- **Time needed**: 3–4 hours
- **Reservation**: **MANDATORY** during peak season — book online at least 3–7 days ahead. Tickets can sell out. Off-peak season: buy 1–2 days ahead.
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:30–18:30 (April–October) / 08:30–18:00 (November–March); last entry 1.5h before closing
- **Tips**: Three pits of different scale and content. Pit 1 is the famous one — a vast hangar containing 6,000+ warriors in formation. Pit 2 is more fragmentary but shows the excavation process. Pit 3 is the command headquarters (smallest but historically interesting). The bronze chariots in the attached exhibition hall are extraordinary and often overlooked. Hire an English audio guide (¥30 + ¥100 deposit) or a licensed English-speaking guide (~¥200-300 for a 2h tour). Get there at 08:30 — the site becomes extremely crowded by 10:00.
- **Ticket**: ¥120 (April–October) / ¥110 (November–March)

#### Xi'an City Wall (西安城墙)
- **Time needed**: 1.5h walk OR 1.5h bike ride for the full circuit
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–22:00 (April–October) / 08:00–20:00 (November–March)
- **Tips**: The most complete ancient city wall in China — 14km perimeter, 12m high, 15m wide at the base. Bikes can be rented at 南门 (South Gate), 北门, and 东门. The full circuit takes ~1.5h by bike and offers alternating views of the modern city outside and the old city within. Going at sunset gives the best light. The 南门 (South Gate) area has the most elaborate architecture and is the recommended starting point.
- **Ticket**: ¥54 (wall entry + bike rental ¥45 for 100min, ¥200 deposit)

#### Muslim Quarter (回民街 / 回坊)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; most food stalls from 08:00; busiest 18:00–21:00
- **Hours**: Always open; food concentrated in the evening
- **Tips**: A dense network of lanes centered around the Great Mosque (清真大寺) in the Huimin community — Muslims have lived in Xi'an since the Tang dynasty along the Silk Road. The lanes are lined with food stalls selling Shaanxi and Northwestern specialties. Best visited in the evening when all stalls are open. The Great Mosque itself (founded 742 CE, rebuilt Ming dynasty) is the most important Islamic site in inland China — enter respectfully; non-Muslim visitors permitted.
- **Ticket**: Muslim Quarter free; Great Mosque ¥25

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Shaanxi History Museum (陕西历史博物馆)
- **Time needed**: 2–3 hours
- **Reservation**: Required — free tickets released online at 09:00 daily for the next day; sell out within minutes during peak season. Arrive early at the gate if unable to book online (limited same-day tickets available from 09:15).
- **Closed**: Monday
- **Hours**: 09:00–17:30 (last entry 17:00); extended hours in summer
- **Tips**: One of China's finest provincial museums — exceptional Tang dynasty gold and silver, Han bronzes, and Silk Road artifacts. The Tang Tri-Color Glazed Pottery (唐三彩) section is a highlight. Allow time for the special exhibition galleries (separate ticket required, ¥20-50 extra). Free entry for the permanent collection.
- **Ticket**: Free (permanent collection); special exhibitions ¥20-50

#### Big Wild Goose Pagoda (大雁塔)
- **Time needed**: 1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–19:00 (pagoda exterior); pagoda interior 09:00–17:00
- **Tips**: A 652 CE Tang-dynasty Buddhist pagoda built to house sutras brought from India by the monk Xuanzang (of Journey to the West fame). Seven stories, 64m tall. The surrounding plaza has a large musical fountain show (free) at 09:00, 12:00, 15:00, and 21:30. The climb to the top gives a good city panorama. The evening fountain show is a popular local gathering.
- **Ticket**: Park free; pagoda interior ¥50

#### Bell Tower & Drum Tower (钟楼 / 鼓楼)
- **Time needed**: 45 min (both combined)
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:30–21:30
- **Tips**: The Bell Tower stands at the geographic center of the old city. The Drum Tower (100m west) has a daily drum performance schedule. Both have good views of the surrounding streets. Best to visit both in one stop — they are connected via the underground Muslim Quarter approach. Evening is lively with the surrounding night market.
- **Ticket**: ¥35 (combined ticket for both)

### Tier 3: If you have more time

## Food & Dining

Xi'an is the gateway to northwestern Chinese cuisine — hearty, wheat-based, and influenced by Central Asian Silk Road exchange.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Mutton pita soup (羊肉泡馍) | Torn flatbread soaked in rich mutton broth; the diner tears the bread themselves (a ritual) | 同盛祥, 老孙家 — both near the Drum Tower; allow 30min for the tearing ritual |
| Chinese burger (肉夹馍) | Crispy flatbread stuffed with slow-braised spiced pork; the world's oldest sandwich | Every street stall in the Muslim Quarter; Rou Jia Mo is best eaten on the street, not in a restaurant |
| Cold skin noodles (凉皮) | Cold wheat or rice noodles in chili oil and vinegar; refreshing and intensely flavored | Muslim Quarter stalls and side-street shops citywide |
| Biangbiang noodles (油泼面) | Wide, hand-pulled noodles topped with dried chili, then doused with boiling oil poured tableside — the oil sizzle is part of the experience | Specialist 油泼面 restaurants; ask for 正宗陕西面 |
| Sweet persimmon cake (柿子饼) | Soft cakes made from Xi'an's famous persimmons; filled with red bean or walnut | Muslim Quarter stalls; seasonal (best Oct–Nov) |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Muslim Quarter (回坊)**: The essential food zone — all of the above in one area; best for dinner
- **Bell Tower / Drum Tower area**: Mix of local restaurants and tourist-oriented options
- **Yanta district**: Less touristy restaurants near Shaanxi History Museum

## Practical Tips for Xi'an

- **Terracotta Warriors are 40km away**: Many first-time visitors are surprised by this. Factor a full half-day minimum including transport. Do not schedule it as a late-afternoon activity
- **Great Mosque etiquette**: Cover shoulders and knees; remove shoes before entering prayer halls; do not visit during prayer times (especially Friday midday); photography of worshippers is not permitted
- **Muslim Quarter food safety**: Quality varies significantly — choose stalls with visible cooking and high turnover; avoid pre-made items sitting in unrefrigerated display cases
- **Shaanxi History Museum reservations**: Free tickets are genuinely difficult to get during peak season. Set a reminder to book online at 09:00 the day before; alternatively, queue at the gate at 09:00 for same-day standby tickets (limited)
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable

## Gotchas
- **Terracotta Warriors "guide" touts**: Unlicensed guides approach foreigners at the site entrance offering tours. Use a booked English audio guide or a licensed guide arranged through your hotel — unlicensed guides are often inaccurate
- **羊肉泡馍 takes 20 minutes to prepare**: You tear the bread; the kitchen soaks it in broth. This is a full meal ritual, not fast food. Do not order it if you're in a hurry
- **Shaanxi History Museum is closed Monday**: A common planning mistake — the museum is one of the best in China and worth building your schedule around
- **Huashan crowds on weekends**: Huashan is extremely popular with Chinese visitors — weekends have long cable car queues (2+ hours). Visit midweek if possible
- **Xi'an summers**: July–August temperatures reach 38°C+ with significant humidity. The Terracotta Warriors site has minimal shade — visit at 08:30 opening and be finished by 11:00
