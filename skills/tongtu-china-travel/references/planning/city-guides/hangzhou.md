# Hangzhou

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2-3 (2 for West Lake + city, 3 to add tea plantations and canal)
- Best season: March–April (spring — cherry blossoms around West Lake) and October–November (autumn foliage, clear skies, hairy crab season); avoid July–August (heat and humidity) and major holidays (West Lake is extremely crowded during Golden Week)
- Must-see: West Lake morning walk or boat ride, Lingyin Temple, Longjing tea village
- Best area to stay: Lakeside (湖滨) — walkable to West Lake, good metro access; Xihu Tiandi area for upscale options
- Key constraint: West Lake has no entrance fee but the scenic area is enormous — a single circuit is 15km. Plan specific zones rather than "visiting West Lake" as a single item. Lingyin Temple requires advance ticket booking on weekends.

## City Orientation

Hangzhou is built around West Lake — a 6.5km² UNESCO-listed freshwater lake surrounded by wooded hills, causeways, pagodas, and temples. The lake is the organizing principle of the city; everything else is peripheral. The city is 45min by 高铁 from Shanghai and is commonly visited as a day trip, but 2 nights allows a more considered experience.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **西湖 West Lake** | Lake circuit, causeways (苏堤/白堤), pagodas, boat rides | Line 1 to 龙翔桥站 or 凤起路站 (lakeside) |
| **灵隐 Lingyin** | Lingyin Temple, Feilai Peak carvings, tea hills | Line 7 to 古翠路站 then bus; or Didi |
| **龙井 Longjing** | Longjing Tea Plantation, tea villages | Didi ~20min from lake; no direct metro |
| **运河 Grand Canal** | Gongchen Bridge area, canal museum, Xiaohe Street | Line 5 to 打铁关站 |
| **上城 Shangcheng** | Hefang Street, Qinghe Fang heritage area | Line 1 to 定安路站 |

### Getting around
- **Metro**: 5 lines. Alipay QR code. ¥2-7 per ride. Line 1 is the backbone for tourist areas.
- **Shared bikes**: Extensive public bike-share network (哈罗单车, Alipay-linked) — the best way to navigate the West Lake area. Flat terrain around the lake. ¥1.5 per 30 min.
- **Electric boats**: Tourist boats crossing West Lake depart from multiple piers; useful for crossing to Gushan Island or mid-lake pavilions (~¥20-45 depending on route).
- **Didi**: Essential for Longjing tea village and Lingyin Temple if not taking the bus.
- **To Shanghai**: 高铁 from 杭州东站 to 上海虹桥 (~45min, ¥80-110).
- **To Suzhou**: 高铁 from 杭州东站 (~1.5h).

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### West Lake (西湖) — UNESCO World Heritage
- **Time needed**: 2–4 hours (partial circuit); full day for complete 15km circuit
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open 24 hours (outdoor)
- **Hours**: Lakeside pavilions and park areas typically 06:00–21:00
- **Tips**: West Lake is best understood as a collection of specific sites within a large scenic area, not a single attraction. Key sub-sites:
  - **苏堤 (Su Causeway)**: 2.8km tree-lined causeway through the lake; most beautiful in spring. Bike or walk.
  - **白堤 (Bai Causeway)**: Shorter 1km causeway to Gushan Island; good views back to the city.
  - **三潭印月 (Three Pools Mirroring the Moon)**: Island with stone pagodas on the lake surface; accessible by tourist boat only.
  - **断桥残雪 (Broken Bridge)**: Famous bridge at the north end of Bai Causeway; best in winter after snow, but always photographed.
  - **雷峰塔 (Leifeng Pagoda)**: Hilltop pagoda on the south shore; reconstructed 2002; lake views from the top.
- **Ticket**: Lake free; Leifeng Pagoda ¥40; tourist boats ¥20-45 per route

#### Lingyin Temple (灵隐寺) + Feilai Peak (飞来峰)
- **Time needed**: 2.5–3 hours
- **Reservation**: Recommended on weekends — book online to avoid same-day queues
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 07:00–18:15 (last entry 17:30)
- **Tips**: One of China's ten most important Buddhist temples, founded 326 CE, with an unbroken active monastic community. The adjacent Feilai Peak has over 300 Song and Yuan dynasty Buddhist cliff carvings — the smiling Maitreya Buddha carved into the rock face is the most famous image. Visit Feilai Peak before the main temple complex. Arrive before 09:00 on weekends to avoid tour groups. The temple still functions as a working monastery; participate respectfully (incense available, ¥10-30).
- **Ticket**: ¥45 (includes both Feilai Peak and Lingyin Temple)

#### Longjing Tea Village (龙井茶园)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily; tea-picking season March–April
- **Hours**: Tea houses and farms typically 09:00–18:00
- **Tips**: The source of China's most famous tea — Dragon Well (龙井) green tea, grown on terraced hillsides above the lake. Walk into the village and visit a tea farm directly — families sell directly and will pour multiple tastings. Longjing tea prices vary enormously (¥100–2,000/100g depending on grade and harvest date); first-spring harvest (明前龙井, before Qingming Festival in April) is the highest grade. Do not buy pre-packaged tea at the lakeside tourist shops — go to the village.
- **Ticket**: Free (village access); tea purchase optional

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Grand Canal — Gongchen Bridge Area (运河 / 拱宸桥)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily (outdoor); museums on the canal closed Monday
- **Hours**: Canal always open; China Grand Canal Museum 09:00–17:00 (closed Monday)
- **Tips**: The southern terminus of the 1,794km Grand Canal (UNESCO). The Gongchen Bridge (拱宸桥, 1631 CE) is the best-preserved section. The surrounding Xiaohe Street (小河直街) is a restored canal-side district with traditional warehouses, teahouses, and local restaurants — less touristy than the lakeside. The Canal Museum is free and contextualizes the canal's importance well.
- **Ticket**: Outdoor free; Canal Museum free

#### Hefang Street (河坊街 / 清河坊)
- **Time needed**: 1–1.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: Shops 09:00–22:00
- **Tips**: A Qing-dynasty commercial street restored with traditional pharmacy buildings, old brand shops, and street food. Less curated than Xintiandi in Shanghai. Worth visiting for: 知味观 (traditional Hangzhou snacks), 胡庆余堂 (century-old traditional medicine shop, free museum inside), and Hangzhou silk products.
- **Ticket**: Free

### Tier 3: If you have more time

| Attraction | Time needed | Notes |

## Food & Dining

Hangzhou cuisine (杭帮菜) is one of China's Eight Great Regional Cuisines — mild, sweet, and focused on freshwater fish and seasonal vegetables. Very different from Sichuan or Cantonese.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| West Lake sweet and sour fish (西湖醋鱼) | Freshwater grass carp poached and served in sweet vinegar sauce; the defining Hangzhou dish | 楼外楼 (Louwailou — on Gushan Island, the historic reference; reserve ahead), most lakeside restaurants |
| Dongpo pork (东坡肉) | Slow-braised pork belly named after the poet Su Dongshi; rich, gelatinous, served in a clay pot | 知味观 (Zhiweigu — traditional chain, multiple locations), lakeside restaurants |
| Longjing shrimp (龙井虾仁) | Freshwater shrimp sautéed with fresh Longjing tea leaves; a spring seasonal dish (March–May only) | Upscale restaurants; ask explicitly during season |
| Beggar's chicken (叫化鸡) | Whole chicken stuffed with aromatics, wrapped in lotus leaves and clay, baked underground | Order in advance (24h notice required); 知味观 and traditional restaurants |
| West Lake lotus root soup (莼菜羹) | Delicate broth with water shield (莼菜) and freshwater shrimp; intensely local | Lakeside restaurants in season (spring/autumn) |
| Hangzhou steamed pork dumplings (小笼包) | Similar to Shanghai but slightly larger; juicy pork filling | 知味观 citywide |

**Dining areas by zone:**
- **Lakeside (湖滨路)**: Higher prices, good quality for Hangzhou specialties; 楼外楼 is the institution
- **Hefang Street**: Street snacks — 葱包桧 (deep-fried spring onion rolls), 定胜糕 (red bean rice cakes), 糖桂花 (osmanthus candy)
- **Xiaohe Street (小河直街)**: Canal-side local restaurants; authentic and affordable

## Practical Tips for Hangzhou

- **West Lake is enormous**: 15km to circumnavigate by foot takes 4+ hours. Use shared bikes (¥1.5/30min, Alipay-linked) for the causeways — they are flat and perfect for cycling
- **Longjing tea pricing**: First-spring harvest (明前龙井) costs ¥500-2,000/100g for genuine high-grade tea. If a price seems too low (under ¥100/100g at a farm), it is likely a lower-grade machine-picked autumn harvest or blended tea. Ask the farmer when it was picked.
- **Golden Week crowds**: West Lake during National Day (October 1–7) is one of China's most crowded destinations — visitor numbers exceed capacity and the experience is significantly degraded. Avoid.
- **Mist and rain**: Hangzhou in spring has frequent light rain and morning mist over the lake. This actually enhances the scenery; bring a light waterproof layer rather than treating rain as a problem
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable

## Gotchas
- **Lingyin Temple on weekends**: Without a pre-booked ticket, the queue can be 45–90 minutes. Book online the day before — the temple sells out of same-day entry slots by 09:00 on holiday weekends
- **楼外楼 (Louwailou) requires advance booking**: This is the most famous restaurant in Hangzhou; walk-ins on weekends will be turned away. Reserve at least one day ahead for lunch or dinner
- **West Lake boat touts**: Unlicensed boatmen at the lakeside offer private boat rides at prices 3-5× the official tourist boat. Use the official pier only (marked with 官方码头)
- **Longjing tea outside harvest season**: Outside March–May, you cannot see tea being picked, and prices for fresh tea are lower. The village is still worth visiting for the landscape and tastings year-round
- **High-speed rail from Shanghai**: The most convenient station in Hangzhou for West Lake is 杭州站 (not 杭州东站) — closer to the lake by 20min taxi. Check which station your train uses
