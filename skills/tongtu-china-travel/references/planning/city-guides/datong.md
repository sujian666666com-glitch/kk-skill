# Datong

## TL;DR
- Recommended days: 2 (1 focused day for Yungang Grottoes + city, 1 for Hanging Temple day trip)
- Best season: May–October (avoid winters — bitterly cold, some sites reduced hours; avoid July–August peak crowding at Yungang)
- Must-see: Yungang Grottoes (非看不可), Hanging Temple (悬空寺) as a day trip
- Best area to stay: Datong Ancient City area (大同古城) — walkable to city sites, convenient for day trips
- Key constraint: Datong is a mid-tier city with limited English — hire a local driver or guide for Yungang and the Hanging Temple day trip. Public transport to these sites is inconvenient.

## City Orientation

Datong was the capital of the Northern Wei dynasty (386–534 CE), which commissioned the Yungang Grottoes — one of the three great Buddhist cave complexes in China. The city was also a major coal mining center; recent investment has restored the ancient city walls and surrounding heritage sites. It is rarely visited by foreign tourists, making the experience more authentic and less crowded than major tourist cities.

### Key zones for tourists

| Zone | What's there | Metro access |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| **大同古城 Ancient City** | Restored city wall, Nine Dragon Screen, Huayan Temple, old streets | No metro; taxi or walk within the walled area |
| **云冈 Yungang** | Yungang Grottoes (UNESCO) | ~30min by taxi from city center; no direct public metro |
| **浑源 Hunyuan** (county, 75km east) | Hanging Temple (悬空寺), Hengshan Mountain | ~1.5h by car; requires a separate day trip |
| **应县 Yingxian** (county, 70km south) | Yingxian Wooden Pagoda (应县木塔, 1056 CE) | ~1h by car; can combine with Hanging Temple |

### Getting around
- **Taxi/Didi**: Primary transport. Didi works in Datong. Taxis are inexpensive (¥15–30 within the city). For Yungang and day trips, arrange a driver for the day (~¥300–500 for a full day including waiting time).
- **Walking**: The ancient city is compact and walkable once you're inside the walls.
- **Metro**: Datong has no metro system. All inter-site transport is by taxi or arranged vehicle.
- **To Yungang from city**: Taxi ¥30–40 each way, or Tourism Bus No. 3 from 大同火车站 (¥4, ~40min).
- **To Hanging Temple**: Private car or booked tour strongly recommended. 1.5h each way; public buses exist but are infrequent.

## Attractions — Details & Logistics

### Tier 1: Must-see

#### Yungang Grottoes (云冈石窟) — UNESCO World Heritage
- **Time needed**: 2.5–3.5 hours
- **Reservation**: Buy tickets online in advance; peak season (May, Oct) can sell out by mid-morning
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:30–17:30 (April–October) / 09:00–17:00 (November–March); last entry 1 hour before closing
- **Tips**: 252 caves carved into sandstone cliffs between 460–525 CE, containing over 51,000 Buddhist statues. The scale is overwhelming — the largest seated Buddha in Cave 20 is 13.7m tall. Start from Cave 5–6 (the most ornate), then walk west to the earlier caves (1–4) for the largest figures. Caves 16–20 are the oldest and most powerful. Photography is permitted in most caves (no flash). Audio guide available in English at the entrance.
- **Ticket**: ¥120 (April–October) / ¥100 (November–March)

#### Hanging Temple (悬空寺) — Day Trip
- **Time needed**: 1–1.5 hours at the site; full day including transit
- **Reservation**: Required — book online in advance; daily visitor quota strictly enforced
- **Closed**: Open daily (may close in severe weather)
- **Hours**: 08:00–18:00 (summer) / 08:30–17:30 (shoulder season)
- **Tips**: A 6th-century temple built into a sheer cliff face above a gorge, 75m above the ground. Supported by horizontal wooden beams inserted into the rock. Defies easy explanation — it must be seen to be understood. The site accommodates visitors in timed groups along suspended walkways (some sections are narrow — not suitable for those with severe vertigo). Combine with Hengshan Mountain (恒山) and Yingxian Wooden Pagoda on the same day trip if driving.
- **Ticket**: ¥130 (+ Hengshan entry ¥53 if combined)

### Tier 2: Highly recommended

#### Datong Ancient City Walls (大同古城)
- **Time needed**: 1.5–2 hours (wall walk) + 30 min for the interior
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–18:00
- **Tips**: One of the best-preserved Ming city wall circuits in northern China — 7.2km perimeter, completely walkable on top. The walls were comprehensively restored in the 2010s. The four city gates with their adjacent corner towers are particularly impressive. The interior of the ancient city has been partially restored with traditional storefronts; the residential areas inside are more authentic.
- **Ticket**: ¥50 (wall access)

#### Huayan Temple (华严寺)
- **Time needed**: 1 hour
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:00–18:00
- **Tips**: A large Liao-dynasty Buddhist temple complex (11th century) with two separate halls — Upper Huayan (上华严寺) and Lower Huayan (下华严寺). The wooden sutra library (薄伽教藏殿) in the lower hall contains original Liao-dynasty clay sculptures of exceptional quality. One of the most important early Chinese wooden architecture sites in existence.
- **Ticket**: ¥60

#### Nine Dragon Screen (九龙壁)
- **Time needed**: 20 min
- **Reservation**: Not required
- **Closed**: Open daily
- **Hours**: 08:30–18:00
- **Tips**: A 45.5m-long glazed tile screen wall built in 1392 — the longest and oldest of China's three famous nine dragon screens (larger than the ones in Beijing's Forbidden City and Beihai Park). The colored glaze tiles still retain their original vivid pigmentation. Quick visit but genuinely impressive up close.
- **Ticket**: ¥25

### Tier 3: If you have more time

| Attraction | Time needed | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Yingxian Wooden Pagoda (应县木塔) | Half day (including transit) | The world's oldest surviving wooden pagoda (1056 CE, Liao dynasty); 67m tall, entirely wood without nails. 70km south; combine with Hanging Temple day trip |
| Datong Museum (大同博物馆) | 1.5 hours | Well-designed museum covering Northern Wei and Liao dynasty history; free; closed Monday |
| Shanhua Temple (善化寺) | 45 min | Tang-to-Jin dynasty temple; less visited than Huayan; good carved figures |

## Food & Dining

Datong is in Shanxi Province, home of China's most important noodle culture.

| Dish | What it is | Where to find |
|------|-----------|---------------|
| Knife-shaved noodles (刀削面) | Thick irregular noodles shaved directly from a block of dough into boiling water; served in broth or with sauce | Every restaurant in the city; this is the non-negotiable local dish |
| Datong braised pork noodles (大同炸酱面) | Thicker noodles with a pork and fermented soybean sauce; different from the Beijing version | Local noodle shops in the ancient city |
| Hunyuan cold noodles (浑源凉粉) | Transparent mung bean noodles served cold with vinegar, chili, and sesame; found only in Hunyuan County | Order while visiting the Hanging Temple area |
| Steamed yellow rice cake (黄糕) | Sticky millet cake, steamed and eaten with stewed vegetables or meat; a Shanxi staple | Traditional restaurants in the ancient city |
| Oat noodles (莜面) | Dense pale noodles made from oat flour; served cold or in soup | Specialty restaurants; a northern Shanxi staple |

**Dining areas:** Ancient city main street (大东街) and surrounding lanes concentrate the better local restaurants. Avoid hotel restaurants for authentic food.

## Practical Tips for Datong

- **English is minimal**: Very few English menus, signs, or staff outside of major hotels. Datong receives few foreign tourists — bring a translation app and use AliTrip's camera translation feature extensively
- **Arrange transport in advance**: Datong lacks the Didi density of larger cities. Ask your hotel to arrange a driver for Yungang and the Hanging Temple day trip the night before
- **Coal city air quality**: Datong has historically had significant air pollution from coal mining and transport. Check AQI before outdoor plans — if AQI exceeds 150, the Yungang cliff faces will appear hazy in photos
- **Cold winters**: November–March temperatures regularly drop below -10°C. Yungang Grottoes in winter are beautiful but extremely cold; dress accordingly
- **Water**: Tap water not drinkable

## Gotchas
- **Yungang caves have quotas**: Daily visitor numbers are capped. Book tickets online at least 2–3 days ahead in peak season (May Golden Week, October National Holiday) — same-day tickets may not be available
- **Hanging Temple is narrow and high**: The walkways are suspended hundreds of meters above the gorge floor, with sections less than 1m wide. This is not suitable for anyone with a serious fear of heights or vertigo
- **Hunyuan 浑源 is a county town, not a city**: Lunch options are limited — the 浑源凉粉 specialty restaurants near the Hanging Temple are your main option. Do not expect variety
- **Datong Museum closes Monday**: Plan accordingly if your city day falls on a Monday
- **Noodle shops close early**: Many traditional noodle shops serve only until 14:00 and reopen for dinner from 17:30. Do not arrive at 15:00 expecting lunch service
