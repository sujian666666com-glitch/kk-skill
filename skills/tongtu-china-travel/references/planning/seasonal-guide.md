# When to Visit China — Seasonal & Holiday Guide

## TL;DR
- **Best overall months**: April, May, September, October (mild weather, manageable crowds outside Golden Week)
- **Avoid if possible**: Oct 1-7 (National Day), May 1-5 (Labor Day), Chinese New Year (Jan/Feb) — extreme crowds and prices
- **Each city has its own best season** — Beijing in autumn, Guilin in spring, Harbin in winter
- **Weather varies dramatically** — tropical south vs. freezing north; always check the specific city

## Month-by-Month Overview

| Month | Weather (general) | Crowd level | Price level | Notes |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| **Jan** | Cold north (-10°C), mild south (10-15°C) | Low (pre-CNY) | Low | Good deals, but cold; CNY falls late Jan or Feb |
| **Feb** | Cold north, mild south | **Extreme** (CNY week) then low | **Peak** (CNY) | Avoid CNY week; late Feb is quiet and cheap |
| **Mar** | Warming, rainy south | Low-medium | Low-medium | Cherry blossoms in Wuhan/Nanjing/Kunming; some rain |
| **Apr** | Pleasant nationwide | Medium | Medium | Spring flowers; comfortable temperatures everywhere; **Canton Fair in Guangzhou — hotel prices surge** |
| **May** | Warm, pre-summer | **High** (May 1-5) then medium | **High** (holiday) | First 5 days = Labor Day rush; after May 5 is great |
| **Jun** | Hot, rainy south (plum rain) | Medium | Medium | Shanghai/Hangzhou humid; north is pleasant |
| **Jul** | Hot everywhere (30-38°C) | High (summer holiday) | High | Students traveling; Chongqing/Nanjing/Wuhan are extreme heat cities; consider high-altitude escapes |
| **Aug** | Hottest month | High (summer holiday) | High | Same as July; typhoon risk on southeast coast |
| **Sep** | Cooling, dry | Medium | Medium | Excellent month — summer crowds gone, weather improving |
| **Oct** | Crisp autumn | **Extreme** (Oct 1-7) then medium | **Peak** (Golden Week) | Oct 1-7 = worst week of the year; after Oct 8 is superb; **Canton Fair in Guangzhou — hotel prices surge** |
| **Nov** | Cool north, mild south | Low | Low | Beijing autumn foliage; good deals; early winter in north |
| **Dec** | Cold north, cool south | Low | Low | Cheapest month; Harbin ice festival starts late Dec |

| City | Best months | Why | Avoid |

## Holiday Calendar & Impact

### Major holidays — PLAN AROUND THESE

| Holiday | 2026 Dates | Duration | Impact level | What happens |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|-------------|
| **Chinese New Year (春节)** | Feb 17 - Feb 23, 2026 | 7 days | ★★★★★ Extreme | Transport sells out 2-3 weeks ahead; many restaurants/shops closed; tourist sites either empty (everyone's at home) or packed (temple fairs); prices +100% on transport |
| **Labor Day (五一)** | May 1-5, 2026 | 5 days | ★★★★☆ Severe | All attractions at daily capacity; hotels +50-100%; train tickets sell out 1-2 weeks ahead |
| **National Day / Golden Week (十一)** | Oct 1-7, 2026 | 7 days | ★★★★★ Extreme | The worst week for tourism — everything is overcrowded, overpriced, and overbooked; 800 million+ domestic trips |
| **Dragon Boat (端午)** | May 31, 2026 | 3 days | ★★★☆☆ Moderate | Short break; popular destinations busier than usual |
| **Mid-Autumn (中秋)** | Oct 6, 2026 | 3 days (merged with National Day in 2026) | — | Merged into Golden Week in 2026 |
| **Qingming (清明)** | Apr 5, 2026 | 3 days | ★★☆☆☆ Mild | Slight uptick in domestic travel; minimal impact on foreign tourists |

### City-specific events that affect hotel prices

| Event | City | When | Impact |
|-------|------|------|--------|
| **Canton Fair (广交会)** | Guangzhou | April (Phase 1: ~Apr 15-19, Phase 2: ~Apr 23-27) and October (~Oct 15-19, 23-27) | ★★★★★ Extreme — hotel prices 2-3× normal citywide; rooms sell out months ahead. This is NOT a national holiday but affects Guangzhou as severely as Golden Week. Travelers not attending the fair are equally affected. Consider staying in Shenzhen (30-min high-speed rail) if visiting Guangzhou in April or October. |

### School holidays (domestic crowd driver)
- **Summer**: July 1 - August 31 — families travel, attractions busier, prices +20-30%
- **Winter**: Late January - late February — overlaps with Chinese New Year

### If you MUST travel during a holiday

1. Book transport and hotels **3-4 weeks ahead** minimum
2. Reserve all attraction tickets the moment they open (usually 7 days prior)
3. Arrive at attractions **before 08:30** — lines are shortest at opening
4. Choose less-famous alternatives: Mutianyu > Badaling, Huangshan villages > main peak, lesser-known West Lake gardens > Su Causeway
5. Consider cities that are NOT top-10 destinations (Quanzhou, Changsha, Luoyang, Dali)
6. Accept that your experience will be different — embrace the festive atmosphere rather than fighting the crowds

## Weather Packing Guide

### What to pack by season

| Season | North China (Beijing, Xi'an, Datong) | Central (Shanghai, Hangzhou, Chengdu, Nanjing) | South (Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Guilin) |
|--------|--------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| **Spring (Mar-May)** | Layers: light jacket + sweater, windy; Datong can have sandstorms in March | Light layers, umbrella (rain) | T-shirt + light jacket, umbrella |
| **Summer (Jun-Aug)** | T-shirt, shorts, sunscreen, hat | Same + umbrella (sudden rain); extra cooling layers for Chongqing/Nanjing extreme heat | Same, very humid — moisture-wicking fabric |
| **Autumn (Sep-Nov)** | Jacket → winter coat by Nov; Datong turns cold by October | Light jacket → medium coat by Nov | T-shirt → light jacket by Nov |
| **Winter (Dec-Feb)** | Heavy coat, thermals, gloves, hat (-10 to -20°C; Datong can reach -20°C) | Medium coat, scarf (0-8°C; Nanjing colder than Shanghai) | Light jacket (10-18°C) |

### Universal packing items for China
- **Comfortable walking shoes** — you will walk 15,000-25,000 steps/day at attractions; Chongqing requires extra grip for steep terrain
- **Portable phone charger** — QR payments drain battery; a dead phone = no payment
- **Umbrella / rain jacket** — weather changes fast, especially in south/central China
- **Sunscreen + hat** — UV is strong, especially at altitude (Yunnan, Tibet)
- **Light cash backup** — ¥200-500 in case of phone/payment issues
