# Alipay for Foreign Tourists

## TL;DR
- Register with any phone number (any country) + passport
- Bind international credit/debit cards: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, UnionPay
- No Chinese bank account required
- Transaction limits: single $5,000 USD equivalent / annual $50,000 USD equivalent
- Pay via QR code at stores, restaurants, transit, attractions — covers 95%+ of daily scenarios in China
- English Service Hotline: +86-571-2688 6000

## Before You Arrive
- You can download, register, and bind your card **before arriving in China** — strongly recommended
- Complete setup while you still have your home data/WiFi to avoid struggling at the airport
- After landing: connect to free airport WiFi (available at all major Chinese airports) if you haven't set up yet
- Consider buying an eSIM in advance through AliTrip inside Alipay, so you have data the moment you land
- Notify your bank before departure that you'll be making transactions in China — this prevents your card from being blocked

## Prerequisites
- Passport (valid)
- International credit or debit card (Visa / Mastercard / American Express / JCB / Diners Club / Discover / UnionPay)
- Phone number with SMS capability (any country)
- Smartphone: iOS 14+ or Android 8+

## Step-by-Step Setup

### 1. Download Alipay
- Search "Alipay" in App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
- Or visit the official website: alipay.com

### 2. Register
- Open the app, tap **"Sign up"**
- Select your country/region code and enter your phone number
- Verify via SMS code

### 3. Identity Verification
- Follow the on-screen prompt to verify your identity
- You will need your passport for this step
- This is a one-time process at registration — subsequent payments do not require re-verification for small amounts

### 4. Add a Bank Card
- On the home screen, tap **"Me"** (我的) → **"Bank Card"** (银行卡) → **"Add"** (添加)
- Or find the **"Bank Card"** section on the home page and tap **"Add Now"**
- Enter your card number and follow the on-screen instructions
- Your bank may trigger a 3D Secure verification on first binding

## How to Pay

### Method 1: Scan the merchant's QR code (Active Scan)
1. Open Alipay, tap **"Scan"** (扫一扫) on the home screen
2. Point your camera at the merchant's QR code
3. Confirm the amount and complete payment

### Method 2: Show your QR code (Passive Scan)
1. Tap **"Pay/Receive"** (收付款) on the home screen
2. Present your payment QR code to the merchant
3. The merchant scans your code to complete the transaction

**Tip**: Match the QR code with the right app — scan Alipay-logo QR codes with Alipay, not with other apps.

## Use Cases

### Paying in stores & restaurants
Most merchants in Chinese cities accept Alipay QR payment. Look for the blue Alipay logo at checkout.

### Public transport (metro & bus)
- Open Alipay → search **"乘车码"** (Transit QR Code) or find it on the home page
- Present the code at the metro gate or bus scanner
- Supported in most major cities (Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, Hangzhou, Chengdu, etc.)

### Booking travel on AliTrip
- Open Alipay → search **"飞猪出行"** (AliTrip) or find it in mini-programs
- Book flights, hotels, train tickets, and eSIM cards
- Supports passport-based booking for foreigners

### Taxi / ride-hailing
- Open Alipay → search **"打车"** (Taxi) to access Didi and other services
- Or download Didi separately and link Alipay as payment method

### Online purchases
- Alipay is accepted on Taobao, Tmall, and many Chinese e-commerce sites

## Transaction Limits & Fees

| Item | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single transaction | $5,000 USD equivalent |
| Annual cumulative | $50,000 USD equivalent |

**Fees**:
- Transactions ≤ ¥200: no fee
- Transactions > ¥200: 3% service fee
- Exchange rate is determined by your card organization (Visa/Mastercard network rate) — check your card statement for the exact rate applied

**Fee tip**: Most everyday spending (meals, coffee, convenience stores, metro) is under ¥200, so the 3% fee rarely applies in daily use. For larger purchases (hotels, shopping), consider paying with your physical credit card directly to avoid the fee.

**Limit note**: Limits are denominated in USD equivalents and converted at the prevailing exchange rate. The RMB amount you can spend per transaction will fluctuate with the exchange rate.

## FAQ

### What cards can I add?
Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, and UnionPay (中国银联) cards issued in any country.

### Do I need a Chinese bank account?
No. You can use Alipay with only an international card. A Chinese bank account is optional and unlocks higher transaction limits.

### What if my card is declined?
- Ensure your bank allows international online transactions
- Some banks block transactions in China by default — call your bank before departure to authorize China transactions
- Turn off VPN before binding or paying — VPN can trigger Alipay's security checks
- Don't retry too many times in a row — repeated failures may temporarily lock your account
- Try a different card brand if one fails
- If your account gets frozen due to risk control, contact the English hotline: +86-571-2688 6000

### Is there a spending limit?
Yes: $5,000 USD equivalent per transaction, $50,000 USD equivalent per year for overseas card accounts. These limits are for daily consumer payments only (no transfers or investments).

### How do I know the exchange rate?
The exchange rate is provided by the card organization (e.g. Visa, Mastercard) and your issuing bank. The rate is shown on the payment confirmation screen in Alipay. For the exact amount charged, refer to your bank's billing statement.

### Can I get a refund?
Refunds are processed back to your original card. The 3% service fee is also refunded proportionally. Processing time depends on your bank (typically 3-15 business days).

### Can I switch the app language to English?
Yes: tap **"我的"** (Me) → **"设置"** (Settings) → **"语言"** (Language) → select English.

### Can I use Alipay for online shopping?
Yes, on platforms like Taobao and Tmall. However, overseas card accounts cannot be used for person-to-person transfers or financial products.

### Do I need a VPN to use Alipay?
No. Alipay works without a VPN in China. In fact, having a VPN active can cause payment failures or trigger security alerts. Turn off your VPN when using Alipay.

## Gotchas
- First transaction after binding a new card may take a few extra seconds for bank authorization
- Some very small vendors (street food, rural areas) may only accept WeChat Pay — have both apps ready
- Your payment QR code refreshes every 60 seconds for security — don't screenshot it
- If you see "payment environment abnormal" error, update the app or try again on a different network
- Transaction limits are per card account, not per device — binding the same card on another phone shares the same quota
