# Payment in China — What Foreign Tourists Need to Know

## TL;DR
- **Mobile QR payment** (Alipay / WeChat Pay) is the dominant method — used by 95%+ of merchants
- **Foreign credit cards** (Visa/Mastercard tap or swipe) work at some large merchants, but coverage is limited
- **Cash** is accepted everywhere by law, but many places prefer mobile payment — carry ¥200–500 as backup
- **Recommended setup**: Install Alipay first (easiest), then add WeChat Pay as backup, plus carry one physical card and some cash

## Payment Methods at a Glance

| Method | Coverage | Setup effort | Best for |
|--------|----------|-------------|----------|
| **Alipay** (QR code) | ★★★★★ | Medium (app + card binding) | Daily spending — stores, restaurants, transit, taxis |
| **WeChat Pay** (QR code) | ★★★★★ | Medium (app + card binding) | Same as Alipay; some small vendors only accept WeChat |
| **Foreign credit card** (tap/swipe) | ★★☆☆☆ | None | Hotels, upscale restaurants, large chain stores |
| **Cash (RMB)** | ★★★★☆ | Low (ATM withdrawal) | Backup, street food, rural areas, small vendors |
| **Apple Pay / Google Pay** | ★☆☆☆☆ | None | Very limited — only select international chain stores |
| **E-CNY** (digital yuan) | ★★☆☆☆ | Medium (app + pilot cities) | Tech-curious travelers in pilot cities |

## Alipay vs WeChat Pay — Which Should I Get?

**Start with Alipay, add WeChat Pay if you can.** Alipay is easier to set up and covers most payment needs. WeChat Pay is a valuable addition but has a harder registration process.

### Why Alipay first?
- Simpler registration — no need for another user to verify your account
- Built-in AliTrip for booking flights, hotels, trains, and eSIM
- Covers 95%+ of daily payment scenarios on its own

### When WeChat Pay adds value
- **Small vendors in smaller cities** — in major cities (Shanghai, Beijing, Guangzhou, etc.) most merchants accept both Alipay and WeChat Pay; in smaller cities, some vendors may only accept WeChat Pay
- **Scan-to-order at restaurants** — most restaurants support both Alipay and WeChat for table QR ordering, but occasionally you'll encounter one that only works via WeChat mini-program
- **WeChat mini-program ecosystem** — some attractions, museums, and local services (bike sharing, queuing) run mini-programs inside WeChat that have no Alipay equivalent
- **Communicating with locals** — WeChat is China's dominant messaging app; if you need to message hotels, tour guides, or Chinese friends, having WeChat Pay in the same app is a natural convenience

### Side-by-side comparison

| | Alipay | WeChat Pay |
|---|--------|-----------|
| Registration difficulty | Easy (phone + passport) | Harder (often needs existing user to verify) |
| Card brands supported | Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, UnionPay | Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, UnionPay |
| Transaction limit (single) | $5,000 USD equivalent | $5,000 USD equivalent |
| Transaction limit (annual) | $50,000 USD equivalent | $50,000 USD equivalent |
| Built-in travel booking | Yes (AliTrip) | No |
| Mini-program ecosystem | Limited | Extensive (ordering, reservations, local services) |
| Messaging | No | Yes (China's dominant chat app) |
| English UI | Yes | Yes |
| Fee (overseas card) | ≤¥200 free; >¥200 charges 3% | ≤¥200 free; >¥200 charges 3% |

**Bottom line**: Set up Alipay first — it's your primary payment tool. Then set up WeChat Pay when you have time, as a complement for scenarios Alipay can't cover.

## Foreign Credit & Debit Cards

### Where they work
- International hotel chains (Marriott, Hilton, IHG, etc.)
- Upscale restaurants and department stores
- Airport shops and duty-free
- Some large supermarkets (Walmart, Carrefour, Ole')

### Where they usually don't work
- Small restaurants, street food, local shops
- Metro/bus systems (QR code or transit card only)
- Taxis (QR code or cash only)
- Most e-commerce (Taobao, Meituan)

### Accepted networks
- **Visa** and **Mastercard**: most widely accepted among foreign cards
- **UnionPay**: accepted at all POS terminals in China (if your home bank issues UnionPay cards, these work seamlessly)
- **JCB**, **Diners Club**, **Discover**: accepted at some merchants
- **American Express**: limited acceptance

### Contactless (tap to pay) / Apple Pay / Google Pay
Visa/Mastercard contactless tap works at some POS terminals, but coverage is far lower than QR code payment. Apple Pay and Google Pay have very limited merchant support in China. **Do not rely on these as your primary payment method.**

## Cash

### Do I need cash?
Yes, carry ¥200–500 as backup. While mobile payment dominates, cash is useful for:
- Places with poor phone signal
- Very small vendors who only accept cash or WeChat
- Emergencies (phone dead, app issues, account frozen)
- Note: China has no tipping culture — locals never tip, and offering a tip may cause confusion or awkwardness

### How to get RMB cash
- **ATMs**: Withdraw from ATMs displaying Visa/Mastercard/UnionPay logos at airports, bank branches, and some convenience stores
- **Currency exchange counters**: Available at airports (port of entry) and major bank branches — look for the **"EXCHANGE"** sign
- **Hotels**: Some hotels and currency exchange kiosks offer exchange services

### Currency basics
- Currency: Renminbi (RMB), also called Chinese Yuan (CNY)
- Symbol: ¥
- Notes: ¥1, ¥5, ¥10, ¥20, ¥50, ¥100
- You can convert remaining RMB back at the airport or port of exit before departure

## International E-Wallets Accepted in China

Some international e-wallets can be used directly at merchants in China without downloading a new app. However, **Alipay or WeChat Pay remain the best options** — treat your home wallet as a backup channel.

| Country / Region | Wallets | Network |
|-----------------|---------|---------|
| Hong Kong | AlipayHK, WeChat Pay HK | Alipay+ / WeChat |
| Macao | MPay | Alipay+ |
| South Korea | Kakao Pay, Naver Pay, Toss Pay | Alipay+ |
| Malaysia | Touch 'n Go eWallet | Alipay+ |
| Thailand | TrueMoney | Alipay+ |
| Singapore | OCBC, Changi Pay | Alipay+ |
| USA (and expanding) | PayPal | WeChat Pay |

**Notes**:
- **WeChat Pay HK**: Link your HK bank account or credit card, scan at WeChat Pay merchants — HKD auto-converts to RMB.
- **AlipayHK**: Scan to pay at any merchant displaying the "Alipay" or "Alipay+" logo.
- **PayPal**: Scan WeChat Pay merchant QR codes directly from your PayPal app — no Chinese account needed, real-time exchange rate.

Check with your e-wallet provider for the latest China payment availability.

## E-CNY (Digital Yuan)

E-CNY is China's official digital currency, available in pilot cities including Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Chengdu, and others. Foreign tourists can download the **"e-CNY"** (数字人民币) app and register with a passport.

This is not essential for most tourists — Alipay and WeChat Pay cover the same merchants. Consider it only if you're interested in trying the technology.

Customer service hotline: +86-10-6619 6136

## Recommended First-Day Checklist

Before departure:
1. Download Alipay — register, bind card, and buy eSIM through AliTrip (priority)
2. Download WeChat — register and bind card (may need a friend to help verify)
3. Notify your bank you'll be traveling to China

Upon arrival:
4. Connect to airport WiFi if you haven't completed setup
5. Withdraw ¥500 from an airport ATM as backup cash
6. Make your first small QR payment with Alipay (convenience store, coffee) to confirm everything works

You're all set.
