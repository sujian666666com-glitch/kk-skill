# WeChat Pay for Foreign Tourists

## TL;DR
- **Recommended as your second payment app** — set up Alipay first (easier), then add WeChat Pay
- Register with any phone number (any country) + ID document (passport, etc.)
- Bind international credit/debit cards: Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, UnionPay
- No Chinese bank account required
- Transaction limits: single $5,000 USD equivalent / annual $50,000 USD equivalent
- Adds value for: some small-city vendors that only accept WeChat, attraction/service mini-programs, and messaging Chinese contacts
- English Service Hotline: +86-571-95017

## Prerequisites
- Valid ID document (passport, HK/Macao/Taiwan travel permits, or foreign permanent resident ID)
- International credit or debit card (Visa / Mastercard / American Express / JCB / Diners Club / Discover / UnionPay)
- Phone number with SMS capability (any country — does not need to be a Chinese number)
- Smartphone: iOS 14+ or Android 8+

## Before You Arrive
- Download and register WeChat before your trip — complete card binding while you have home WiFi
- **Important**: New WeChat accounts often require an existing WeChat user to scan a QR code to verify your registration. Ask a friend who uses WeChat to help, or do this step well before your trip — don't wait until the airport
- Notify your bank before departure that you'll be making transactions in China
- If you already use WeChat for messaging, you can add payment to your existing account — no new registration needed

## Step-by-Step Setup

### 1. Download & Register
- Search "WeChat" in App Store (iOS) or Google Play (Android)
- Open the app, tap **"Sign Up"**
- Select your country/region, enter your phone number, and verify via SMS

### 2. Find WeChat Pay
- Tap **"Me"** (我) → **"Pay & Services"** (支付与服务) → **"Wallet"** (钱包)

### 3. Add a Bank Card & Verify Identity
- In **"Wallet"** (钱包), tap **"Credit/Debit Cards"** (信用卡/扣账卡) → **"Add Card"** (新增卡)
- Review and agree to the WeChat Pay User Service Agreement & Privacy Policy
- Fill in your ID information as prompted (passport number, name, etc.)
- Tap **"Next"** (下一步) to add your card and follow the on-screen instructions
- This completes both identity verification and card binding in one flow

## How to Pay

### Method 1: Scan the merchant's QR code (Active Scan)
1. Open WeChat, tap **"+"** at the top right → **"Scan"** (扫一扫)
2. Point your camera at the merchant's QR code
3. Confirm the amount and complete payment

### Method 2: Show your QR code (Passive Scan)
1. Tap **"Me"** (我) → **"Pay & Services"** (支付与服务) → **"Pay/Receive"** (收付款)
2. Present your payment QR code to the merchant
3. The merchant scans your code to complete the transaction

**Tip**: Match the QR code with the right app — scan WeChat-logo QR codes with WeChat, not with Alipay.

## Use Cases

### Paying in stores & restaurants
Most merchants in Chinese cities accept WeChat Pay. Look for the green WeChat Pay logo at checkout.

### Public transport (metro & bus)
- Open WeChat → search **"乘车码"** (Transit QR Code) in mini-programs
- Present the code at the metro gate or bus scanner
- Availability varies by city

### Taxi / ride-hailing
- Open WeChat → search **"滴滴出行"** (Didi) mini-program
- Or download Didi separately and link WeChat Pay as payment method

### Mini-programs (小程序)
WeChat's unique advantage over Alipay for daily life in China. Many local services run as mini-programs inside WeChat:
- **Restaurant ordering**: Scan a table QR code to view menu, order, and pay — no waiter needed
- **Queue & reservation**: Take a number at popular restaurants or book time slots at attractions
- **Attraction tickets**: Some scenic spots and museums require WeChat mini-program booking
- **Bike sharing**: Meituan Bike, Hellobike, etc.

To access: scan a merchant's QR code or search the service name in WeChat's search bar.

### Messaging + Payment
- WeChat is China's dominant messaging app — if you're chatting with Chinese contacts (hotels, tour guides, friends), you can send/receive messages and pay in one app

## Transaction Limits & Fees

| Item | Limit |
|------|-------|
| Single transaction | $5,000 USD equivalent |
| Annual cumulative | $50,000 USD equivalent |

**Fees**:
- Transactions ≤ ¥200: no fee
- Transactions > ¥200: 3% service fee
- If you request a refund, the service fee is refunded proportionally along with the transaction amount
- Exchange rate is determined by your card organization and issuing bank — check your card statement for the exact rate

**Fee tip**: Most everyday spending (meals, coffee, convenience stores, metro) is under ¥200, so the 3% fee rarely applies in daily use.

**Limit note**: Limits are denominated in USD equivalents and converted at the prevailing exchange rate. The RMB amount you can spend per transaction will fluctuate with the exchange rate.

## FAQ

### What ID documents can I use?
Six types are accepted: passport, HK/Macao Mainland Travel Permit, Taiwan Mainland Travel Permit, HK/Macao Resident Permit, Taiwan Resident Permit, and Foreign Permanent Resident ID.

### What cards can I add?
Visa, Mastercard, American Express, JCB, Diners Club, Discover, and UnionPay (中国银联) cards.

### Does my phone number need to be Chinese?
No. Any phone number that can receive SMS verification codes will work.

### What can I pay for with an overseas card?
Daily consumer purchases in mainland China (stores, restaurants, transport, etc.). Person-to-person transfers (red packets, transfers) are not supported with overseas cards.

### Is there a service fee?
Yes: transactions over ¥200 are charged a 3% fee. Transactions of ¥200 or less are free. If you get a refund, the fee is also refunded proportionally.

### What if my card is declined?
- Ensure your bank allows international online transactions
- Notify your bank before departure to authorize China transactions
- Turn off VPN before binding or paying — VPN can trigger security checks
- Don't retry too many times in a row to avoid temporary account lock
- Try a different card brand

### How do I switch to English?
Tap **"Me"** (我) → **"Settings"** (设置) → **"General"** (通用) → **"Language"** (多语言) → select English.

### Do I need a VPN to use WeChat Pay?
No. WeChat and WeChat Pay work without a VPN in China. Turn off your VPN when making payments to avoid issues.

### I can't register — it asks someone to verify me?
WeChat requires an existing user to scan a QR code to verify new accounts. Ask a friend, colleague, or family member who has WeChat to help. Some hotels in China can also assist. This is the most common blocker for new users — do it before your trip if possible.

## Gotchas
- **Registration verification is the #1 pain point** — new accounts almost always need an existing WeChat user to help verify. Plan ahead
- WeChat Pay setup is bundled with identity verification — you do both when adding your first card
- Some merchants display both Alipay and WeChat QR codes side by side — scan the one matching your app
- Your payment QR code refreshes frequently for security — don't screenshot it
- WeChat Pay and WeChat messaging use the same app — no separate download needed
- Transaction limits are per account, not per device
- When you scan a restaurant table QR code, it may open a mini-program in Chinese — use WeChat's built-in translate feature (long press text → Translate) to read it
