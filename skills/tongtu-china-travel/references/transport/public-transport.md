# Metro & Public Bus in China

## TL;DR
- Alipay Transport QR code is the easiest way to ride the metro — works in all major cities
- Foreign contactless cards (Visa/Mastercard tap) also work in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Chengdu
- All major city metros have English signage and bilingual announcements
- Public buses are cheap but harder to navigate — metro is the better choice for tourists

## How to Pay for Metro

### Option 1: Alipay Transport QR Code (Recommended)

The simplest method. Open Alipay > Transport tab > activate the QR code for your city > scan at the turnstile to enter and exit.

- Works in: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Chengdu, Hangzhou, Xi'an, and 40+ other cities
- **You must activate separately for each city** — tap "Switch City" in the Transport tab
- Bus QR codes may also require separate activation in some cities
- Fee: same as local metro fare, charged via your linked Visa/Mastercard

### Option 2: Contactless Tap (Visa/Mastercard)

Tap your foreign contactless credit or debit card directly at the turnstile.

- Available in: Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu — coverage expanding but varies by station, confirm at the turnstile
- No app needed — just tap and go
- **Note**: Fares may be slightly higher than Alipay QR in some cities (Beijing confirmed)

### Option 3: Single-Trip Tickets

Buy at self-service ticket machines or service counters inside stations.

- **Self-service ticket machines**: accept cash (some only coins, others also bills) and sometimes Alipay/WeChat QR. Foreign cards generally not supported at machines (Beijing is a rare exception)
- **Staffed service counters**: Shanghai accepts Visa, Mastercard, Amex, JCB, Diners Club via POS at all service centers. For other cities, if you need to pay with a foreign card, go to the staffed counter rather than the machine
- Self-service machines support English interface

### Option 4: Transit Cards

Physical reloadable NFC cards — simple, no app needed:

| City | Card name | Where to buy | Deposit |
|------|-----------|--------------|---------|
| Beijing | Yikatong (一卡通) | Any metro station, cash | CNY 20 (refundable) |
| Shanghai | SPTC (上海公共交通卡) | Metro stations, convenience stores | CNY 20 (refundable) |

- iPhone users: Shanghai SPTC can be added to Apple Wallet, but provisioning typically requires a UnionPay card
- These cards also work on buses and some taxis
- **Tip**: Some cards are part of the national "Transportation Union" (交通联合) network and work across multiple cities. Ask the staff before purchasing: "Is this card usable in other cities?" (这张卡能在其他城市用吗？)

## Public Bus

Buses are very cheap (CNY 1-3) but harder to navigate than the metro:

**Payment methods:**
- Alipay Transport QR code (scan on boarding)
- Coins (exact change — no change given, typically CNY 2)
- Transit cards (Yikatong, SPTC, etc.)
- International contactless tap is generally **not** available on buses

**Challenges for foreigners:**
- Route information and stop announcements are often Chinese-only
- No obvious way to know when to get off without navigation app
- Crowded during rush hours

**Tip**: Use Amap, Apple Maps, or **MetroMan** (English-supported, offline-capable app covering nearly all Chinese metro systems) to find routes and track your stop in real time. Or just take the metro.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I bring luggage on the metro?**
Yes, with limits. All bags must go through X-ray security screening at station entrances. Rules vary by city — Beijing's limits are: weight ≤30 kg, length ≤1.8 m, width and height each ≤0.5 m. In practice, standard suitcases (24 inches) are fine; large suitcases (28 inches) fit within limits but are uncomfortable during rush hours.

**What are the operating hours?**
Typical first train: 5:30-6:30 AM. Typical last train: 10:00-11:00 PM. Varies by line and city. Check Amap or the station display for exact times on your line.

**Is the metro safe?**
Very safe. Stations are well-lit, staffed, and monitored. Petty theft is rare but keep valuables close in crowded carriages during rush hours.

**How do I know which exit to use?**
Metro stations have exits labeled A, B, C, D, etc. Check Amap or Apple Maps before you get off — they show which exit is closest to your destination.

**Do children need a ticket?**
Children under 1.3 m tall ride free. Over 1.3 m pays full fare. Same rule applies to buses. Note: your Alipay/WeChat QR code supports one person per ride — if a child needs a ticket, prepare a separate payment method or buy a single-trip ticket.

## Tips

- **Rush hours** (7:30-9:30 AM, 5:00-7:30 PM weekdays): avoid if possible, especially with luggage
- **Shanghai bonus**: look for pink information pillars in stations — scan the QR code for instant translation service
- **Transfer between lines**: follow signs to the line number. Large stations may require 5-10 minutes of walking between platforms
