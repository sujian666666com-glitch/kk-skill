# Intercity Transport in China

## TL;DR
- **High-speed rail (HSR) is the best way to travel between Chinese cities** — fast, punctual, comfortable, affordable
- Foreigners can buy tickets on **12306** (English interface, passport registration) or **Trip.com** (accepts foreign cards)
- No paper tickets needed — show your passport at the gate (use the staffed lane)
- Long-distance buses are only useful for destinations not on the HSR network

## High-Speed Rail

### Why Take the Train?

China's HSR network is the world's largest — 45,000+ km connecting 200+ cities. For distances under 1,200 km, HSR is faster than flying (no airport waits) and far more comfortable than driving.

### Train Types

| Prefix | Type | Top speed |
|--------|------|-----------|
| **G** | High-speed (高铁) | 350 km/h (217 mph) |
| **D** | Fast train (动车) | 250 km/h (155 mph) |
| **C** | Intercity (城际) | 200 km/h (124 mph) |

G-trains are the fastest and most common for long-distance. D-trains are slightly slower but often cheaper. C-trains serve short intercity routes.

### Journey Times and Pricing

China's HSR network connects most major cities within 2–6 hours. Some common examples:
- **Beijing → Shanghai** (~1,318 km): 4.5–6 hours
- **Beijing → Xi'an** (~1,200 km): 4–5.5 hours
- **Shanghai → Hangzhou** (~180 km): under 1.5 hours
- **Guangzhou → Shenzhen** (~110 km): 30–45 minutes

**For current schedules, train numbers, and real-time prices**, always use the `flyai search-train` command rather than relying on reference figures — fares vary by train, date, and seat class.

### How to Buy Tickets

#### Option 1: 12306 App/Website (Official)

- Full English interface — download "12306" app or visit 12306.cn
- Register with **passport number + email** (no Chinese phone number required)
- **Identity verification required**: upload passport photo + selfie online (takes 3-5 days), or verify instantly at any station ticket counter with your passport
- Payment: international credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Diners Club, Discover), Alipay, WeChat Pay
- Tickets released **15 days before departure** — book early for popular routes
- Service hours: 05:00-01:00 (ticket sales); refunds nearly 24/7

#### Option 2: Trip.com or AliTrip (Easiest for foreigners)

- English-friendly, accepts **international credit cards** (Visa/Mastercard/JCB/Amex) and **PayPal**
- Handles identity verification on your behalf
- Slightly higher service fee (~CNY 20-40 per ticket)
- **Recommended if you can't complete 12306 verification before your trip**

#### Option 3: Station Ticket Counter

- Bring your passport. Write down: train number, date, departure and arrival stations
- No Chinese required — staff can usually process the request with written information
- Available for walk-up purchases, but popular routes may sell out

### Station Entry Process

1. **Arrive 30-45 minutes early** (especially at large stations like Beijing South or Shanghai Hongqiao)
2. **Enter the station**: show passport at the entrance. Some stations have automated passport readers; others have a staffed lane for foreign passports
3. **Security screening**: bags go through X-ray scanner, walk through metal detector
4. **Find your waiting area**: check the departure board for your train number and waiting room
5. **Board**: arrive at the gate 15-30 minutes early. Boarding typically starts 3-5 minutes before departure. Automated gates usually don't support foreign passports — use the staffed lane
6. **No paper tickets**: fully e-ticketed since 2020 — your passport IS your ticket

### Seat Classes

| Class | Layout | Space | Features |
|-------|--------|-------|----------|
| **Second Class (二等座)** | 3+2 seating | Standard — similar to economy air | Power outlet, fold-down tray, decent legroom |
| **First Class (一等座)** | 2+2 seating | More legroom, wider seats | Power outlet, adjustable headrest |
| **Business Class (商务座)** | 2+1 or 1+1 | Fully flat seats | Lie-flat, meal included, lounge access (some stations) |

**Recommendation**: Second Class is excellent value and perfectly comfortable for trips under 5 hours. First Class is worth the upgrade for 4+ hour journeys.

### Onboard Amenities
- Free WiFi on some trains (coverage varies)
- Hot water dispenser in each carriage (bring your own cup or buy drinks)
- Food cart service with snacks, instant noodles, and boxed meals (CNY 15-60)
- Scan the QR code at your seat to order food via WeChat/Alipay (available on many trains)
- Dining car on longer-distance trains
- Clean restrooms (Western-style toilets available)

## Long-Distance Bus

### When to Use

Buses are only useful for destinations **not served by HSR**:
- Remote scenic areas (e.g., access roads to Zhangjiajie, Jiuzhaigou)
- Small towns between cities
- Overnight budget travel (sleeper buses)

For any city-to-city route served by HSR, the train is faster, more comfortable, and similarly priced.

### How to Buy Tickets
- Station ticket counters (passport required)
- Trip.com or Alipay (limited route coverage)
- Payment: cash or Alipay at station counters

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I change or refund my ticket?**
Yes. Via 12306 app or at station counters.
- **Changes**: one free change per ticket (date, train, seat class, or destination can all be changed). Additional changes incur fees.
- **Refunds**: fees depend on timing — 5% if 48+ hours before departure, 10% if 24-48 hours, 15% if 24 hours or less, 20% if after departure.

**What if I miss my train?**
Your ticket is void. You'll need to buy a new ticket. Some stations allow same-day standby for the next train on the same route, but this is not guaranteed.

**Can I bring luggage on the train?**
Yes, and there's no strict weight limit for passengers. Overhead racks and space behind the last row of seats are available. Large suitcases fit fine. No checked baggage system.

**Is food available?**
Yes — food cart service, dining car (on long-distance trains), and platform vendors at major stations during stops. Prices are higher than outside, so consider buying food before boarding.

**Do trains run on time?**
Chinese HSR is extremely punctual — average delay is under 1 minute. Delays of 10+ minutes are rare and usually weather-related.

**Can children ride for free?**
Children under 14 must be accompanied by an adult. One adult can bring one child for free (no separate seat). If you need a seat for the child or are bringing more than one child, purchase a child discount ticket. Declare the child in 12306 or Trip.com after booking to link them to the itinerary for boarding.

## Tips

- **Book popular routes early**: Beijing-Shanghai, Shanghai-Hangzhou, and Guangzhou-Shenzhen sell out quickly during holidays (Spring Festival, National Day, Labor Day)
- **Check station names carefully**: major cities have multiple stations (e.g., Beijing has Beijing South, Beijing West, Beijing North). Confirm which station your train departs from
- **Use flyai for search**: the skill's `flyai search-train` command can search schedules and compare options — see SKILL.md routing table
