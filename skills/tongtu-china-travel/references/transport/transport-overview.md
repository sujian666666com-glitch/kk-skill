# Getting Around in China — What Foreign Tourists Need to Know

## TL;DR
- **Metro** is the cheapest and most reliable way to get around major cities — use Alipay QR code to enter
- **Didi** (ride-hailing) is the most convenient door-to-door option — has English interface, online payment only (foreign cards, Alipay, WeChat — set up before your trip)
- **High-speed rail** is the best way to travel between cities — fast, comfortable, and affordable
- **Recommended setup**: Install Alipay (for metro/bus/Didi) + Amap (for navigation) before your trip

## Transport Payment — The Basics

**Alipay QR code is the universal key.** With Alipay set up (see `../payment/alipay-for-foreigners.md`), you can pay for:
- Metro (all major cities)
- Public bus (most cities)
- Didi rides (via Alipay mini-program)
- High-speed rail tickets (via AliTrip)

**Foreign contactless cards (Visa/Mastercard tap)** work at metro turnstiles in Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, and Chengdu — but may cost slightly more than Alipay QR.

**Cash** is accepted for metro ticket machines, some buses (exact change only), and street taxis. Carry CNY 50-100 in small bills as transport backup.

## Navigation Apps

| App | English support | Best for | Notes |
|-----|----------------|----------|-------|
| **Amap (高德地图)** | Yes (English version available) | Navigation, transit routes, ride-hailing | Best overall choice for foreigners — accurate maps, built-in ride-hailing |
| **Apple Maps** | Yes | Familiar interface, basic navigation | Uses Amap data — good accuracy, but less detail for last-mile directions |
| **Google Maps** | N/A | Not recommended | Blocked in China without VPN; data is outdated even with VPN |
| **Baidu Maps** | Minimal | Not recommended for non-Chinese speakers | Very detailed but Chinese-only interface |

**Recommendation**: Install **Amap** + **Apple Maps** before departure. Use Apple Maps for familiar UX, switch to Amap for ride-hailing and detailed local directions.

## Practical Tips

- **Peak hours**: 7:30-9:30 AM and 5:00-7:30 PM on weekdays — metro is packed, Didi prices surge. Allow extra time.
- **Prepare destinations in Chinese**: Save Chinese names/addresses of your hotels, attractions, and restaurants. Show Chinese text to drivers or paste into apps for better accuracy.
- **Security screening**: All metro stations and train stations require bags to go through X-ray scanners. Allow 5-10 extra minutes at stations.
- **No tipping**: Tipping is not practiced in China — do not tip taxi drivers or any transport staff.
