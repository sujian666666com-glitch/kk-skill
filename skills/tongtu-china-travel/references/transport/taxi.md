# Taxi & Ride-Hailing (Didi) in China

## TL;DR
- **Use Didi** — it's China's Uber, has a full English interface, and accepts foreign credit cards
- Street taxi hailing has mostly disappeared — app-based ride-hailing is dominant
- Easiest shortcut: use the Didi mini-program inside Alipay (no extra registration needed)
- English destination search works for major places; copy-paste Chinese text from a map app for less well-known locations

## Didi — The Primary Choice

### What Is Didi?
Didi (滴滴) is China's dominant ride-hailing platform (70%+ market share) — think Uber. It operates in 400+ cities nationwide, 24/7, and is the most foreigner-friendly transport app in China.

### How to Get Started

**Option A: Standalone Didi App (Best experience)**
1. Download **"DiDi China"** from App Store / Google Play (not "DiDi Rider" — that's for other countries)
2. Register with your international phone number (SMS verification required)
3. Set language to English on first launch
4. Add payment: Account > Wallet > Payment Methods > add Visa/Mastercard/JCB/Diners Club
5. You're ready to ride

**Option B: Didi via Alipay (Easiest)**
1. Open Alipay > search "Didi" or go to Transport tab
2. Use the Didi mini-program — no separate registration or ID verification needed
3. Payment goes through your Alipay-linked card

### Payment Methods

**Didi standalone app** — most flexible:
- Directly add foreign cards in-app: Visa, Mastercard, JCB, Diners Club (Account > Wallet > Payment Methods)
- Also supports Alipay, WeChat Pay, Apple Pay
- Auto-Pay recommended (max CNY 500 per ride deducted automatically)

**Didi mini-program** (inside Alipay/WeChat) — easiest to start:
- Payment goes through the host platform (Alipay mini-program = Alipay; WeChat mini-program = WeChat Pay)
- Foreign cards work fine — just make sure your card is already linked in Alipay or WeChat before you ride

### Ride Types
- **Express** (快车) — budget option, most common
- **Comfort** (舒适型) — newer cars, more legroom
- **Premier** (优享) — premium vehicles, professional drivers

Prices increase in that order. Express covers most needs.

### Key Features for Foreigners
- Full English interface (map, ride options, driver info, receipts)
- Bilingual real-time chat translation with drivers
- 24/7 English customer service
- Safety features: trip sharing, 110 emergency alarm, in-car audio recording, phone number privacy protection

## Didi vs. Street Taxi

| | Didi | Street Taxi |
|---|------|------------|
| **Availability** | Nationwide, 24/7, suburban areas | Major city streets only, hard to find in rain/rush hour |
| **Pricing** | Dynamic — cheaper off-peak, surges at peak | Metered — fixed daytime rate, night surcharge +20-30% (23:00-05:00) |
| **Payment** | App only (foreign cards, Alipay, WeChat) | Cash or mobile pay |
| **English** | Full English interface | None — driver speaks Chinese only |
| **Invoices** | E-invoice (fapiao) in app | Paper receipt from meter |
| **Verdict** | **Use this by default** | Backup when phone is dead |

**Note**: As of 2026, street taxi hailing has largely disappeared in practice. App-based hailing is the norm across China.

## How Much Does It Cost?

### Street Taxi (metered)
- **Base fare**: CNY 10-16 (covers the first 2-3 km), then CNY 2-3 per km — varies by city
- **Night surcharge**: +20-30% between 23:00-05:00 (varies by city)
- **Typical cost**: short trip within a district (3-5 km) ≈ CNY 15-25; cross-district (10-15 km) ≈ CNY 35-60
- **Airport transfers**: see `airport-transfer.md` for city-by-city prices

### Didi (ride-hailing)
- **Price estimate shown before you confirm** — you know the cost upfront
- **Check detailed pricing rules in-app**: Account > Settings > User Guide — shows base fare and surcharges for your current city
- **Off-peak**: generally 10-20% cheaper than metered taxis
- **Peak / rain / holidays**: dynamic surge pricing — can be 30-50% more expensive than metered taxis
- **Late night**: no fixed surcharge, but fewer drivers means higher dynamic prices

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I tell the driver where I am?**
Pin your exact pickup location on the Didi map. If the driver calls, show your phone screen to a nearby person and ask them to speak to the driver. Didi's in-app translation also helps.

**How do I input my destination?**
- **Type in English** — works well for major destinations (e.g., "The Bund, Shanghai", "Beijing South Railway Station"). Didi will show matching results.
- **For less well-known places**, English search may not find them. Copy-paste the Chinese name from a map app (Amap, Apple Maps), your booking app (Trip.com, Booking.com, Alipay), or a website into Didi's search box.
- **Ask your hotel front desk** to write down your next destination in Chinese — they're used to helping with this.

**How do I identify my driver?**
The app shows the driver's car color, plate number (last 4 digits), and photo. Match these before getting in. You can also show the driver the last 4 digits of your phone number from the app.

**Can I get an invoice (fapiao)?**
Yes. In Didi, go to completed trips > request e-invoice (fapiao). For street taxis, ask the driver for a receipt ("fapiao, 发票") — the meter will print one.

**Can I pay with cash?**
Only traditional taxis accept cash. Didi Express/Comfort/Premier are online payment only. Since China is essentially cashless, even taxi drivers often can't give change — mobile payment is strongly recommended.

**Is ride-hailing safe?**
Very safe. All Didi drivers are licensed and ID-verified. Safety features include: full-trip GPS monitoring, trip sharing with emergency contacts, one-tap 110 police alarm, in-car audio recording, and phone number privacy protection.

## Tips

- **Language barrier with taxi drivers**: Most drivers don't speak English. Show your navigation route on your phone screen — this is the most effective way to communicate your destination. Have your hotel/destination name saved in Chinese text as backup.
- **Order 5-10 minutes early**: give yourself time to get to the pickup point, especially at airports or large venues.
- **Surge pricing**: Avoid Didi during 7:30-9:30 AM, 5:00-7:30 PM, and rainy weather. Fares can double.
- **Toll roads**: If the route includes a highway toll (e.g., airport expressway), you pay the toll (CNY 10-20) on top of the fare.
- **Tipping**: No tipping in China. The fare shown is the fare you pay.
- **Long trips**: For day trips or multi-stop itineraries, consider chartering a car through your hotel — often more cost-effective than multiple Didi rides.
