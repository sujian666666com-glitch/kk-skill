# 144-Hour / 72-Hour Visa-Free Transit

## TL;DR
- Citizens of **54 countries** can transit through China for up to **144 hours (6 days)** without a visa
- You MUST have a confirmed onward ticket to a **third country** (not your origin country)
- You can only stay within the **designated area** of your entry port (usually the city/province)
- Available at **30+ ports** — all major cities covered (Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, Chengdu, Xi'an, Hangzhou, etc.)
- Free, no application needed — just declare at immigration upon arrival

## Who Qualifies

### Eligible nationalities (54 countries)

**Europe (39):**
Albania, Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Czech Republic, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, France, Germany, Greece, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Malta, Monaco, Montenegro, Netherlands, North Macedonia, Norway, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Serbia, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Ukraine, United Kingdom, Russia

**Americas (6):**
Argentina, Brazil, Canada, Chile, Mexico, United States

**Oceania (2):**
Australia, New Zealand

**Asia (7):**
Brunei, Japan, Qatar, Singapore, South Korea, United Arab Emirates, Georgia

### Conditions (ALL must be met)

1. **Valid passport** from one of the 54 eligible countries
2. **Confirmed onward ticket** departing China to a third country/region within 144 hours (or 72 hours at 72h-only ports)
3. **Third country rule**: Your onward destination must be different from your origin country (the country you flew in from)
4. **Enter and exit through designated ports** (see port list below)
5. **Stay within the designated area** — you cannot freely travel to other cities (see area restrictions)

## How It Works

### Step-by-step at the airport

1. **On the plane**: When filling out the arrival card, write "144-hour visa-free transit" as purpose of visit
2. **At immigration**: Go to the regular foreigners' queue (not the visa-free/Chinese citizens lane)
3. **Tell the officer**: "I'm applying for 144-hour visa-free transit"
4. **Show these documents**:
   - Your passport
   - Boarding pass or booking confirmation for your onward flight (to a third country)
   - Your accommodation details in China (hotel name + address)
5. **Get stamped**: The officer stamps a temporary entry permit in your passport — it shows your allowed stay area and deadline
6. **Done** — proceed to baggage claim and customs

### No pre-application needed
Unlike a visa, there is no form to fill out in advance, no fee, and no waiting period. Everything happens at the immigration counter upon arrival.

### 72-hour ports (3-day stay)
Some smaller ports only offer 72 hours. These follow the same rules but with a shorter deadline:
- Changchun, Hohhot, Guiyang, Haikou, and several others

## Area Restrictions — What You Can and Cannot Do

### What "designated area" means
- You are restricted to the city or province listed for your entry port
- Example: If you enter through Beijing Capital Airport, you can travel freely within Beijing, Tianjin, and Hebei — but you **cannot** take a train to Shanghai
- The combined Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei and Shanghai-Jiangsu-Zhejiang zones are very generous for tourists

### Practical implications

| I entered at... | I CAN visit... | I CANNOT visit... |
|-----------------|---------------|-------------------|
| Beijing (PEK/PKX) | Beijing, Great Wall, Tianjin, Chengde | Shanghai, Xi'an, Chengdu |
| Shanghai (PVG/SHA) | Shanghai, Hangzhou, Suzhou, Nanjing, West Lake | Beijing, Guangzhou |
| Guangzhou (CAN) | Guangzhou, Shenzhen, Zhuhai, Shantou | Beijing, Shanghai |
| Chengdu (TFU/CTU) | Chengdu, Leshan Giant Buddha | Jiuzhaigou (outside zone), Xi'an |
| Xi'an (XIY) | Xi'an, Terracotta Warriors, Xianyang | Chengdu, Beijing |

### What if I want to visit multiple cities?
- If the cities are in the **same zone** (e.g., Shanghai + Hangzhou + Suzhou) → OK
- If they're in different zones → you need either a **visa-free entry** (if your country qualifies) or a **regular visa**
- You can also do separate 144h transits on different trips

## Counting the 144 Hours

### When does the clock start?
From **00:00 on the day after arrival**. Example:
- Arrive Monday at 22:00 → clock starts Tuesday 00:00 → must depart before Sunday 24:00
- This gives you a generous window — effectively 6 full days

### When must I leave?
Before your 144 hours expire. The exact deadline is stamped in your passport by the immigration officer.

### What if my onward flight is delayed?
If your departure is delayed due to force majeure (weather, airline cancellation), report to the local PSB immediately. They can extend your stay — but you must report proactively, not after the deadline.

## FAQ

### Does my onward ticket need to be a flight?
No. It can be a flight, train, bus, or ferry — as long as it leaves China to a third country/region. However, a flight is the most commonly accepted proof.

### Can I exit from a different port than I entered?
Yes, as long as both ports are within the same designated area. Example: enter at Shanghai Pudong (PVG), exit at Hangzhou (HGH) — both are in the Shanghai-Jiangsu-Zhejiang zone.

### Can my "third country" be Hong Kong or Macau?
Yes. Hong Kong and Macau count as separate destinations for this policy. A common route: fly into Shanghai from Seoul, transit 144h, fly out to Hong Kong.

### Can I come back for another 144h transit later?
Yes. There's no limit on how many times you use this policy. Each transit is independent.

### What if I miss my onward flight?
Rebook immediately. If you cannot depart before the 144h deadline, go to the local PSB to apply for a regular visa or extension before your time runs out. Do not overstay.

### Can I enter by land/sea?
Some ports include land and sea crossings (e.g., Shenzhen has land ports to Hong Kong). Check the specific port list. Most tourists use airports.

### Do I need a hotel booking?
Immigration may ask for your accommodation address. Have at least your first night's hotel name and address ready (printed or on your phone).

### Can I take a domestic flight within the designated area?
Yes, as long as you stay within the zone. Example: in the Beijing-Tianjin-Hebei zone, you could technically fly between airports within the zone. In practice, trains are more common.

## Gotchas
- **"Third country" rule is strict** — flying Tokyo → Beijing → Tokyo does NOT qualify (same origin and destination). You need Tokyo → Beijing → Seoul (or any different country)
- **Don't leave your zone** — if caught outside the designated area, you face fines and potential detention. GPS on your phone doesn't enforce this, but random checks at hotels and train stations do (hotels register foreign guests with police)
- **Stamp carefully** — the immigration officer writes your deadline by hand. Double-check it's correct and legible
- **Hotels must register you** — Chinese law requires hotels to register foreign guests with local police within 24 hours. Stick to regular hotels (not unregistered Airbnbs) to avoid issues
- **Re-entry resets nothing** — if you leave China and re-enter, you start a fresh 144h period (assuming you meet all conditions again)
- **Tibet is not accessible** — even within your designated area, Tibet requires a separate permit that takes weeks to arrange. Not compatible with 144h transit
