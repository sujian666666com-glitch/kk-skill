# Getting a Chinese SIM Card

## TL;DR
- **China Unicom** is the best choice for foreigners — best English support, most airport counters, best frequency band compatibility with international phones
- **Buy at the airport arrivals hall** — counters are open long hours, staff are used to helping foreigners, takes 15–30 minutes
- **Bring your passport** — real-name registration is required by law (passport + face scan)
- **Tourist plans**: Around ¥100–200 for 20–50GB data valid 15–30 days
- **Main benefit over eSIM**: You get a Chinese phone number — needed for some mini-programs and local services that don't accept foreign numbers (core apps like Alipay, WeChat, Didi work with foreign numbers)

## Which Carrier to Choose?

| Carrier | Foreigner-friendly | Coverage | Int'l phone compatibility | English service | Recommendation |
|---------|-------------------|----------|--------------------------|-----------------|----------------|
| **China Unicom (中国联通)** | ★★★★★ | ★★★★☆ | ★★★★★ | Yes (airport, hotline) | **Best choice for foreigners** |
| **China Mobile (中国移动)** | ★★★☆☆ | ★★★★★ | ★★★☆☆ | Limited | Good coverage but band issues |
| **China Telecom (中国电信)** | ★★☆☆☆ | ★★★★☆ | ★★☆☆☆ | Rare | Not recommended for foreigners |

### Why China Unicom?
- **Band compatibility**: Uses bands that almost all international phones support (FDD-LTE Band 1/3, 5G n78). China Mobile uses TDD-LTE Band 41 and 5G n41/n79 which some foreign phones lack.
- **Airport presence**: Has counters in most major airport arrivals halls specifically for tourist SIM sales
- **English support**: Staff at airport counters typically speak basic English; customer service hotline (10010) has English option
- **Tourist-specific plans**: Offers pre-paid plans designed for short-term visitors

### When China Mobile makes sense
- You're traveling to **remote/rural areas** where China Mobile has better coverage
- Your phone definitely supports Band 41 (check at frequencycheck.com)
- China Unicom counter is unavailable at your arrival airport

### Why not China Telecom?
- Uses CDMA bands for 3G fallback that most international phones don't support
- Fewer foreigner-oriented services
- Less airport presence for tourist SIMs

### City Carrier Stores

If you miss the airport counter or need a different plan later:
- Look for **"中国联通" (China Unicom)** branded stores — usually found on main commercial streets
- Use Amap or Apple Maps to search "中国联通营业厅"
- Bring your passport — same registration process applies
- **Note**: City stores are less accustomed to foreign customers. Use a translation app (e.g., Google Translate, Apple Translate) to communicate — show the screen to staff. Key phrase: "我想办一张短期预付费SIM卡" (I want a short-term prepaid SIM card).

### Online Pre-order (limited availability)
Some carriers offer online ordering for tourist SIMs that can be picked up at the airport or delivered to your hotel. Check China Unicom's international website for availability to your destination.

## Real-Name Registration Process

Chinese law requires real-name registration for all SIM cards. The process for foreigners:

### What you need:
- **Passport** (original — copies not accepted)
- **Your face** (live face scan at the counter)
- **A working phone** that accepts the SIM card

### Process (15–30 minutes):
1. Present passport to counter staff
2. Staff scans your passport information page
3. Complete a **face verification scan** (look at the camera)
4. Choose your plan and phone number (staff will show available numbers)
5. Pay for the plan (cash or Alipay/WeChat if you already have them set up; airport counters usually also accept foreign credit cards)
6. Staff inserts the SIM and activates it
7. Test by making a call or connecting to data
8. Done — you'll receive an SMS confirming activation

### Important notes:
- The SIM is tied to your passport number — one person can register up to 5 numbers
- If you lose the SIM, you'll need to visit a store with your passport to replace it
- Some stores may have difficulty processing non-Chinese passports — airport counters handle this daily and are more reliable

## Frequency Band Compatibility Check

**Before buying a local SIM, verify your phone supports Chinese network bands:**

### Quick check:
1. Go to [frequencycheck.com](https://www.frequencycheck.com)
2. Enter your exact phone model (e.g., "iPhone 15 Pro A2848")
3. Select the carrier (China Unicom or China Mobile)
4. Check the compatibility score — anything above 70% means good basic coverage

### Key bands by carrier:

| Carrier | 4G bands needed | 5G bands (optional) |
|---------|-----------------|---------------------|
| **China Unicom** | Band 1 (2100), Band 3 (1800) | n78 |
| **China Mobile** | Band 41 (2500), Band 1 (2100) | n41, n79 |
| **China Telecom** | Band 1 (2100), Band 3 (1800) | n78 |

**Rule of thumb**: If your phone is a recent flagship (iPhone 12+, Samsung S21+, Pixel 6+), it almost certainly supports all major Chinese bands. Budget phones and older models are the ones to verify.

## How to Top Up / Recharge

If your prepaid balance runs out:

### Via Alipay (easiest)
1. Open Alipay → search "话费充值" (phone recharge)
2. Enter your Chinese phone number
3. Select amount (¥50/100/200)
4. Pay — balance added within minutes

### Via carrier app
- Download "中国联通" app → log in with your number → recharge section

### At convenience stores
- 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson — tell staff "充话费" (chōng huà fèi) and show your number
- Pay cash or scan code

### Via the carrier's WeChat mini-program
- Search "中国联通" in WeChat → official account → recharge

## What to Do When Leaving China

### Short-term visitors (≤30 days):
- **Just leave** — prepaid plans expire naturally. No cancellation needed.
- The number will be recycled after 3 months of inactivity

### If you plan to return:
- **Keep the SIM** and top up ¥10–20 before leaving to prevent auto-cancellation
- Numbers are cancelled after 3 months with zero balance and no activity
- Having a persistent Chinese number is valuable for app registrations

### Want to formally cancel:
- Visit any carrier store with your passport
- Ask to cancel the number ("我要注销这个号码")
- Any remaining balance can sometimes be refunded (varies by carrier)

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does registration take?
15–30 minutes at airport counters. May be longer at city stores if staff are less experienced with foreign passports.

### Can I use the SIM in a portable WiFi device (MiFi)?
Yes — the SIM works in any unlocked device that supports the correct bands. Some tourists buy a SIM and put it in a pocket WiFi to share data among a group.

### Can I keep my Chinese number active from overseas?
Yes, but you'll incur monthly fees even without use (usually ¥5–10/month minimum). Top up via Alipay from overseas to keep it alive.

### Do I need a Chinese phone number to register Alipay?
No. Alipay accepts foreign phone numbers for registration. A Chinese number is NOT required for Alipay.

### What if my phone is carrier-locked?
A carrier-locked phone (common in the US/Japan with contracts) will reject the Chinese SIM. Options:
1. Contact your home carrier to unlock the phone before traveling
2. Use eSIM instead (if supported)
3. Buy a cheap unlocked phone in China (starts at ¥500–800 for a basic smartphone)

### Can I receive SMS verification codes from Chinese services on this number while abroad?
Generally no — most Chinese carriers don't forward domestic SMS internationally by default. Some plans offer international SMS receiving, but it's not guaranteed. If you need to receive Chinese verification codes while abroad, consider keeping international roaming active on the Chinese SIM.

### Is there a deposit?
Some plans require a small deposit (¥50–100) that is applied as credit to your account. Tourist plans at airports typically do not require a separate deposit.
