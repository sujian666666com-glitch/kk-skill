# eSIM for China Travel

## TL;DR
- eSIM is the **easiest way** for short-term tourists to get data in China — no physical card swap, instant activation
- **Buy via Alipay eSIM section or AliTrip mini-program** (recommended) — integrates with your Alipay payment ecosystem
- Most eSIMs are **data-only with no phone number** — you can browse the internet but cannot make calls or receive SMS on the eSIM
- Keep your home SIM active (roaming enabled) to receive OTP verification codes for Alipay, banks, etc.
- eSIM traffic typically routes through international networks → **you can access Google, WhatsApp, Instagram** without extra steps
- Your phone must support eSIM — check before purchasing

## What Is eSIM?

eSIM (embedded SIM) is a digital SIM built into your phone's hardware. Instead of inserting a physical card, you scan a QR code or tap a link to download a carrier profile. You can switch plans or add new ones without opening your phone.

**Key difference from physical SIM**: No card to insert/remove. You "install" a data plan digitally.

## Phone Compatibility Check

### Does my phone support eSIM?

**Most phones released after 2020 support eSIM**, but there are exceptions:

| Brand | eSIM-supported models (examples) |
|-------|----------------------------------|
| **Apple** | iPhone XS and later (all models) |
| **Samsung** | Galaxy S20 and later, Galaxy Z series, Galaxy A54+ |
| **Google** | Pixel 3a and later |
| **Huawei** | P40 Pro+, Mate 40 Pro (varies by region) |
| **Xiaomi** | 12T Pro, 13 series and later (varies by region) |

**How to check:**
- **iPhone**: Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM (if this option exists, you're good)
- **Android**: Settings → Network & Internet → SIMs → Add eSIM / Download SIM
- **Or**: Check your phone model at [esimdb.com](https://esimdb.com) or your manufacturer's spec page

### Important: Carrier lock
If your phone was purchased through a carrier contract (common in the US with AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile), it may be **carrier-locked** and reject other eSIM profiles. Contact your carrier to unlock before purchasing a China eSIM.

### Important: Chinese network bands
Most international phones support Chinese network bands, but some budget models may not. Key bands for China:
- **4G LTE**: Band 1 (2100MHz), Band 3 (1800MHz), Band 41 (2500MHz)
- **5G NR**: n41, n78, n79

You can verify at [frequencycheck.com](https://www.frequencycheck.com) — enter your phone model and select China.

## Installation Steps (General)

### iPhone
1. Go to **Settings → Cellular → Add eSIM**
2. Choose **"Use QR Code"**
3. Scan the QR code provided after purchase (or tap the activation link in email)
4. Label the plan (e.g., "China Data")
5. Set the new eSIM as your **data line** (keep your home SIM for calls/SMS)
6. Done — the plan activates when you arrive in China (or immediately, depending on provider)

### Android (Samsung/Google/Xiaomi)
1. Go to **Settings → Network & Internet → SIMs → Add eSIM** (wording varies by brand)
2. Choose **"Scan QR code"**
3. Scan the QR code from your purchase
4. Follow prompts to add the profile
5. Set the new eSIM as your mobile data connection
6. Done

### Tips
- **Don't delete your home SIM profile** — keep it active for receiving verification SMS (even if you turn off its data)
- **Turn on data roaming** for the eSIM line after installation (some providers require this for activation in China)

## When to Install and Activate

- **Install** (download the profile to your phone): Recommended on departure day, using home WiFi. If you miss this step, you can also install using airport WiFi after landing.
- **Activate** (enable the eSIM line on your phone): Do this after arrival. Most travel eSIMs begin their data countdown when your phone first connects to a Chinese network.
- **Some plans start on purchase/install date** — check the plan terms. If so, buy close to your departure date.

## Pros and Cons

### Pros
1. **No physical card needed** — keep your home SIM in the phone for calls/SMS
2. **Access to international services** — Google, WhatsApp, Instagram all work (home routing)
3. **Instant setup** — scan QR code, ready in minutes
4. **No passport/registration** — buy online from anywhere, no real-name process
5. **Cheaper than roaming** — $2–8/day vs $10–15/day for carrier roaming

### Cons
1. **Data only (most plans)** — cannot make voice calls or receive SMS on the eSIM number
2. **No Chinese phone number** — core apps (Alipay, WeChat, Didi) work with foreign numbers, but some mini-programs and local services require a CN number
3. **Phone must support eSIM** — older or budget phones may not
4. **Data caps** — most plans have limited data (1–20GB); heavy video streaming can exhaust it quickly

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I make phone calls with eSIM?
Most China travel eSIMs are **data-only**. You cannot make or receive regular phone calls. Use VoIP apps (WhatsApp calls, FaceTime, Skype) over data instead. Your home SIM can still receive calls via roaming (charges may apply).

### Can I register Alipay/WeChat with eSIM?
The eSIM itself has no phone number, so you don't register "with" the eSIM. Instead:
- **Alipay**: Register using your **home country phone number**. The OTP verification code is sent to your home number via SMS (received through roaming on your home SIM). No Chinese number needed.
- **WeChat**: Same — register with your foreign number. Some features may be limited without a Chinese number.
- **Didi**: Supports foreign phone number registration — you can use ride-hailing without a Chinese number.
- **Some mini-programs and local services**: May require a Chinese phone number — you'll need a local SIM for these.

**Key point**: Keep your home SIM active with roaming enabled (disable data roaming to avoid charges, but keep voice/SMS roaming on). The eSIM handles your internet; your home SIM handles all OTP codes — including those from Alipay, your home bank, email services, and any other 2FA-protected accounts.

### What if I haven't installed the eSIM before arriving?
You can install using airport WiFi or other public WiFi after landing, then activate immediately. It's smoother to install before departure, but not a hard requirement.

### How much data do I need?
- **Light use** (maps, messaging, email): 500MB–1GB/day
- **Moderate use** (social media, photos, some video): 1–2GB/day
- **Heavy use** (streaming, video calls, uploading): 3–5GB/day

For a 7-day trip with moderate use, a 10GB plan is usually sufficient.

### Can I use eSIM and a local Chinese SIM at the same time?
Yes, if your phone supports **dual SIM** (most modern phones do — one physical SIM slot + one eSIM, or two eSIMs). This gives you both a Chinese number and access to international services: use the eSIM for data (Google, WhatsApp accessible via home routing) and the local SIM for the Chinese number. Most tourists won't need this since Alipay, WeChat, and Didi all work with foreign numbers — but it's a good option if you need a Chinese number for specific services (some mini-programs, bike sharing, etc.).

### What if my eSIM runs out of data mid-trip?
- Most providers allow you to **top up** or buy an additional plan through their app
- Alternatively, switch to WiFi at your hotel until you can purchase more data
- Consider buying a plan with more data than you think you'll need — the cost difference is small

### Is eSIM legal in China?
Yes. eSIM is perfectly legal for tourists in China. The data routes internationally, which is why it bypasses local network restrictions.
