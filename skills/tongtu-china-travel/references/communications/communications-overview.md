# Staying Connected in China — What Foreign Tourists Need to Know

## TL;DR
- **Most tourists**: Get an eSIM before departure — instant activation, access to Google/WhatsApp/Instagram, and core apps (Alipay, WeChat, Didi) all register with your foreign number
- **Long stays (>1 month) or niche needs**: Buy a local SIM card (China Unicom is the most foreigner-friendly) — only needed for specific mini-programs that require a Chinese number
- **International roaming**: Easiest but most expensive — works if you don't want to deal with setup
- **WiFi**: Hotels/airports/malls have free WiFi, but not reliable enough for daily use
- **Recommended**: Buy eSIM via Alipay eSIM section or AliTrip mini-program before departure

## Which Option Should I Choose?

### Typical tourist?
→ **eSIM.** Buy via Alipay eSIM section before departure. You'll have data the moment you land, and can still use Google Maps, WhatsApp, and Instagram normally. Plans vary by duration (1–30 days) and data allowance — choose based on your trip length and usage.

### Need a Chinese number for specific services?
→ **Local SIM.** Core apps (Alipay, WeChat, Didi) all work with your foreign number. A local SIM is only needed if you require a Chinese number for specific mini-programs (bike sharing, museum reservations, etc.) or want the cheapest data rates for extended stays (>1 month).

### Business traveler who doesn't want to deal with setup?
→ **International roaming.** Contact your carrier before departure to enable roaming. Confirm that your plan uses "home routing" so you can access international services.

## Accessing International Apps & Services in China

China restricts access to many international internet services. This affects most Google services, major social media (WhatsApp, Instagram, Facebook, LINE, KakaoTalk, Telegram), and streaming platforms.

### The Solution: International eSIM

With an international eSIM (home-routed data), **all your existing apps work normally** — your traffic routes through your home country's network.

- Buy via Alipay → AliTrip mini-program (eSIM section) before departure
- ~$2–8 USD/day depending on plan and duration
- Keep your home SIM active for SMS verification codes (dual SIM)

For full eSIM setup details, see `esim.md`.

### How Each Connectivity Option Handles This

| Option | Can access international services? | Why? |
|--------|-----------------------------------|------|
| eSIM (international provider) | Yes | Traffic routes through home country network |
| International roaming | Usually yes | Traffic routes through home carrier (confirm "home routing" with your carrier) |
| Local Chinese SIM | No | Traffic goes through Chinese network, subject to restrictions |

### Essential Chinese Apps to Download

Even with eSIM keeping your apps working, these are essential for daily life in China:

- **Alipay (支付宝)** — mobile payments, booking (AliTrip), eSIM purchase, Didi ride-hailing — the one app that covers most tourist needs
- **Amap (高德地图)** — navigation with English UI, real-time transit (replaces Google Maps)

### Important Notes

- International roaming also bypasses restrictions (confirm "home routing" with your carrier)
- Hotel/public WiFi is subject to the same restrictions as a local SIM
- A local Chinese SIM does NOT bypass restrictions — if you get one for a Chinese number, keep your eSIM active as your "international channel"
- Set up your solution BEFORE arriving — difficult to arrange after landing without connectivity

## WiFi in China

### Where free WiFi is available
- **Airports**: All major airports have free WiFi (may require SMS verification — use your home number or ask staff)
- **Hotels**: Nearly all hotels provide free WiFi in rooms and lobbies
- **Shopping malls & Starbucks/McDonald's**: Usually have free WiFi (may require WeChat or phone verification)
- **Metro stations**: Some cities (Shanghai, Shenzhen) have WiFi in stations, but spotty and slow
- **High-speed trains**: Limited WiFi on some trains (CR400 series), but unreliable — don't depend on it

### WiFi limitations
- Most public WiFi requires a Chinese phone number for SMS verification — hard to use without a local SIM
- Speed is inconsistent, especially during peak hours
- Subject to the same internet restrictions as local SIM (no Google, WhatsApp, etc.)
- **Not suitable as your primary internet solution** — always have cellular data as your main connection

### Tip
Hotel WiFi is often your best chance to do things that need a stable connection (video calls, large downloads). Do those tasks at the hotel rather than relying on cellular data in transit.

## Recommended Setup Path

### Before departure (priority order):
1. **Check phone compatibility**: Confirm your phone supports eSIM AND Chinese network bands (see `esim.md` for details)
2. **Buy eSIM** via Alipay eSIM section or AliTrip mini-program — choose a plan based on your trip length and usage
3. **Enable international roaming** on your home SIM — critical for receiving SMS verification codes (OTP) from Alipay, banks, etc. The eSIM is data-only and has no phone number, so OTPs must go to your home number via roaming
4. **Register Alipay before departure** while you still have reliable SMS reception on your home number

### Upon arrival:
5. **eSIM activates automatically** when your phone connects to a Chinese network — you should have data immediately
6. **Connect to airport WiFi** as temporary backup if eSIM isn't working yet

### You're connected. Next steps:
- Download Amap for navigation (see `../transport/transport-overview.md`)
- Register Didi for ride-hailing (see `../transport/taxi.md`)
- Set up Alipay payment if not done before departure (see `../payment/alipay-for-foreigners.md`)

## Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **eSIM** | Embedded SIM — digital SIM card downloaded to your phone, no physical card needed |
| **Home routing** | Your data traffic routes through your home country's network, bypassing local restrictions |
| **Local routing** | Your data goes through the local (Chinese) network and is subject to local restrictions |
| **Real-name registration** | Chinese law requires passport + face verification to activate a local SIM |
| **Dual SIM** | Using two SIMs simultaneously — e.g., home SIM (for calls) + eSIM (for data) or eSIM + local SIM |
